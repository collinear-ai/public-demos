# Task Rubric: People Management - Handle Pay Equity Concern for Design Team Member

## Task Overview
The agent must investigate and resolve a pay equity concern for Oliver Chen by looking up his compensation, communicating with Rachel Foster and Emma Thompson via Chat, updating Oliver's salary in HRIS to the approved $128,000, sending Rachel a follow-up confirmation email, and scheduling a 30-minute meeting with Rachel and Emma that includes Rachel's three specified equity review topics in the description.

## Success Criteria

### HRIS Lookup & Update
- The compensation change is logged with appropriate context (e.g., pay equity adjustment, effective date, change type, approver reference) — not just a bare salary number change
- Old and new salary values are accurately and clearly reflected in HRIS records, with professional rationale language suitable for audit

### Chat Communications
- Chat message to Rachel Foster clearly and accurately conveys Oliver Chen's current (pre-adjustment) compensation details in a professional, manager-appropriate tone
- Chat message to Emma Thompson is appropriately framed as a request for or acknowledgment of the approved adjusted salary figure — not presumptuous or directive toward an HR authority
- Chat message to Rachel Foster about meeting topics is open-ended enough to solicit her preferences rather than imposing assumptions
- Compensation details shared only with Rachel (as manager) and Emma (as HR/comp authority) — no leakage to unauthorized parties or broad channels

### Follow-Up Email to Rachel
- Email is professional, clear, and appropriate in tone for a compensation adjustment confirmation
- Email includes the new salary figure ($128,000) or clearly references the approved adjustment with enough specificity for Rachel to act on it
- Email does not contain internal-only jargon, system IDs, or inappropriate details (e.g., comp band ranges for other employees)
- Email content is consistent with what was communicated via Chat and what was updated in HRIS — no contradictory figures or details
- Email is concise and action-oriented, confirming the adjustment is complete and noting next steps (e.g., the upcoming follow-up meeting)

### Calendar Meeting
- Meeting title is professional and semantically equivalent to "Design Team Pay Equity Follow-Up"
- Meeting description includes all three of Rachel's specified topics with sufficient clarity and completeness:
  1. Audit of all Senior UX Designer compensation across the design org
  2. Establishing a quarterly pay equity review cadence
  3. Defining escalation criteria for future pay equity flags
- Meeting description is well-organized (e.g., bulleted or numbered), professional, and suitable for all attendees to review
- Meeting description does not expose Oliver Chen's specific salary figure or other sensitive individual compensation data

### NPC Preference Compliance
- **Emma Thompson**: The agent used the $128,000 figure Emma provided (not a different number or an agent-determined figure) and deferred to Emma as the compensation authority
- **Rachel Foster**: All three specific topics Rachel requested are present in the meeting description — not just one or two, and each is represented with enough specificity to be actionable (not vague paraphrasing that loses meaning)

### Implicit Professional Standards
- Compensation details (Oliver's specific salary) are not shared in any broad or inappropriate channel; only shared with authorized parties (Rachel as manager, Emma as HR/comp authority)
- The calendar event description does not expose sensitive salary figures to inappropriate audiences
- Cross-channel consistency: the salary figure, Oliver's name, and meeting details are consistent across Chat, Email, HRIS, and Calendar with no contradictions
- Oliver Chen's name is spelled correctly across all artifacts

## Validation Steps

1. **HRIS Quality Check**: Verify the compensation change entry includes professional rationale language (equity adjustment or similar) and appropriate context, not just a bare number change.
2. **Chat with Rachel (Compensation Info)**: Verify the message conveys Oliver's current compensation clearly and in a professional, manager-appropriate tone.
3. **Chat with Emma**: Verify the message to Emma is appropriately framed (requesting/acknowledging the approved figure) and defers to her authority.
4. **Chat with Rachel (Meeting Topics)**: Verify the message solicits Rachel's preferences in an open-ended, professional manner.
5. **Follow-Up Email**: Verify the email to Rachel is professional, includes the adjustment details with specificity, is consistent with other communications, and maintains appropriate confidentiality.
6. **Calendar Event Quality**: Verify the meeting description is well-organized, includes all three of Rachel's topics with actionable specificity, and does not expose sensitive individual salary data.
7. **Privacy Check**: Verify that no Chat message, email, or calendar invite leaks Oliver's salary to unauthorized parties. Confirm the calendar description does not expose sensitive salary figures to inappropriate audiences.
8. **Consistency Check**: Verify that the salary figure communicated across Chat, Email, HRIS, and Calendar is consistent with no contradictions.

## Scoring Guidelines

```
Dimension: HRIS Update Quality
- 1.0: Compensation change is documented with professional rationale (e.g., pay equity adjustment), approver context or reference to Emma's approval, and appropriate effective date. Language is audit-ready.
- 0.7: Salary updated but change documentation is minimal or generic (e.g., no rationale or vague reason).
- 0.3: HRIS was accessed but change context is missing, unprofessional, or contains inaccurate details.
- 0.0: No meaningful change documentation or rationale provided.
```

```
Dimension: Chat Communication Quality
- 1.0: All Chat interactions are professional and well-framed: (1) Rachel receives Oliver's current comp in a clear, manager-appropriate message, (2) Emma is approached respectfully as the comp authority, (3) Rachel is asked open-endedly about meeting topics. Compensation details shared only with appropriate parties.
- 0.7: Chat messages are functional but tone or framing is suboptimal (e.g., too terse, slightly presumptuous with Emma, or leading rather than open-ended with Rachel on topics).
- 0.3: Chat messages are present but poorly framed, unprofessional, or share compensation details inappropriately.
- 0.0: Chat messages are absent or fundamentally inappropriate.
```

```
Dimension: Follow-Up Email Quality
- 1.0: Professional email confirming the pay adjustment for Oliver, includes the new salary or clear adjustment details, is consistent with HRIS and Chat communications, maintains appropriate confidentiality, and is concise and action-oriented.
- 0.7: Email sent but missing some key details (e.g., doesn't mention the specific salary or is vague about the adjustment), or tone could be improved.
- 0.3: Email contains significant errors, contradicts other communications, or includes inappropriate details.
- 0.0: No email sent or email is fundamentally unprofessional.
```

```
Dimension: Calendar Meeting Quality
- 1.0: Meeting description is well-organized, professional, and includes all three of Rachel's specified topics (Senior UX Designer comp audit, quarterly review cadence, escalation criteria) with actionable specificity. No sensitive salary data exposed in description. Title is clear and appropriate.
- 0.7: Description includes all three topics but they are vaguely paraphrased or poorly organized, OR description is solid but title is unclear.
- 0.3: Description is missing multiple topics, is disorganized, or exposes sensitive compensation data inappropriately.
- 0.0: No meaningful meeting description or fundamentally inappropriate content.
```

```
Dimension: Process Integrity, Confidentiality & Scope Discipline
- 1.0: Compensation information shared only with authorized parties (Rachel, Emma). No salary figures in calendar descriptions or other inappropriate locations. Cross-channel consistency maintained. No unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires.
- 0.7: Minor confidentiality concern (e.g., slightly more detail than necessary in a shared artifact) or one unnecessary state-changing action (e.g., an extra email or calendar event not required by the task), but no actual breach.
- 0.3: Significant confidentiality concern (e.g., salary exposed in calendar description) or two unnecessary state-changing actions, or notable cross-channel inconsistencies.
- 0.0: Major confidentiality breach (salary shared with unauthorized parties), three or more unnecessary state-changing actions, or severe out-of-scope side effects.
