# Task Rubric: People Management - Urgent offboarding for long-tenured Operations Analyst

## Task Overview
Handle the immediate offboarding of Ryan O'Connor, a long-tenured Operations Analyst who is resigning effective immediately. This involves sending a farewell email, scheduling an exit interview, coordinating with Nancy Cooper via Chat (acknowledging her frustration and gathering exit interview topics), coordinating with Catherine Moore via Chat (confirming Finance contact for final pay email), sending a final pay coordination email to Catherine with the correct CC, and updating HRIS records.

## Success Criteria

### Farewell Email to Ryan O'Connor
- Acknowledges his long tenure (over a decade / 10+ years in Operations) in a genuine, personalized way — not boilerplate
- Mentions that HR will follow up on final pay details
- Mentions that HR will follow up on COBRA/benefits details
- Professional, warm tone appropriate for a departing long-tenured employee — conveys appreciation and goodwill
- Does not contain internal HR jargon or sensitive internal process details
- Does not over-share about internal offboarding steps, resignation reasons, or other confidential matters

### Exit Interview Calendar Event
- Event title is professional and neutral (e.g., "Exit Interview" or similar appropriate phrasing — not alarming or overly clinical)
- Event description meaningfully incorporates Nancy Cooper's two requested topics: (1) handoff status on Q1 vendor contract renewals Ryan was managing and (2) Ryan's access credentials for the logistics tracking platform that need to be revoked — both should be described clearly enough for attendees to prepare
- Description is well-organized and actionable, giving attendees clarity on what will be discussed
- Description does not contain confidential HR-internal information beyond what attendees need

### Chat DM to Nancy Cooper — Acknowledging Frustration
- Acknowledges the situation and her frustration about Ryan's departure with empathy — acknowledges emotion without matching or amplifying frustration
- Confirms that offboarding is underway with appropriate reassurance
- Professional and empathetic tone — does not dismiss her concerns or sound overly bureaucratic

### Chat DM to Nancy Cooper — Exit Interview Topics
- Agent asks Nancy what specific items she wants covered during the exit interview (not assumed/guessed without asking)
- Agent incorporates Nancy's response (the two specific topics) into the calendar event description, demonstrating active listening and follow-through

### Chat DM to Catherine Moore — Finance Contact Confirmation
- Agent asks Catherine which specific Finance contact should be copied on the final pay coordination email before sending the email
- Message is clear and professional, making it easy for Catherine to respond with the needed information

### Final Pay Coordination Email to Catherine Moore
- Email content clearly addresses coordination of Ryan O'Connor's final pay with enough detail for Catherine and Thomas Reed to act on it
- Professional content appropriate for finance coordination — clear, action-oriented, and concise
- Does not contain sensitive information beyond what is necessary for final pay coordination (no resignation reasons, personal details, etc.)

### HRIS Update
- Ryan O'Connor's HRIS record changes are accurate and audit-appropriate — status, last working date, and departure type are consistent with the resignation scenario
- Language used in any notes or reason fields is professional and policy-safe, specific enough for audit purposes without unnecessary sensitive detail

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- Ryan O'Connor's name is spelled consistently across all artifacts (email, calendar, chat, HRIS)
- Nancy Cooper's and Catherine Moore's names are spelled consistently
- Dates and times are consistent across calendar invites, emails, and chat messages
- Information communicated across channels does not contradict itself (e.g., dates, roles, next steps)

### Privacy & Professionalism
- No sensitive details about Ryan's resignation reasons shared inappropriately across any channel
- Calendar event description does not contain confidential HR-internal information beyond what attendees need
- Final pay details not shared with unauthorized parties
- Farewell email does not expose internal HR processes to the departing employee

### Scope Discipline
- Agent does not perform unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires (e.g., extra emails, extra calendar events, extra chat messages to uninvolved parties, unrelated HRIS changes)
- Additional read-only actions (lookups, searches, exploratory queries) are acceptable and not penalized
- Each confirmed unnecessary state-changing action reduces the score in this dimension

## Validation Steps

1. **Farewell Email Quality**: Review the farewell email to Ryan O'Connor for warmth, professionalism, and completeness — does it genuinely acknowledge his 10+ year tenure, mention final pay follow-up and COBRA/benefits follow-up, and avoid internal jargon or sensitive details?

2. **Exit Interview Event Quality**: Review the calendar event for a professional title, clear and actionable description incorporating both of Nancy's requested topics (Q1 vendor contract renewals handoff and logistics tracking platform credentials revocation), and appropriate level of detail for attendees.

3. **Chat to Nancy — Tone & Empathy**: Review Chat DMs to Nancy Cooper for empathetic acknowledgment of her frustration, confirmation that offboarding is underway, and professional tone that neither dismisses nor amplifies her emotions.

4. **Chat to Nancy — Topic Gathering**: Verify the agent asked Nancy for exit interview topics via Chat before finalizing the calendar event, demonstrating proper sequencing and incorporation of her preferences.

5. **Chat to Catherine — Sequencing & Clarity**: Verify the agent asked Catherine via Chat for the Finance contact before sending the final pay email, and that the message was clear and professional.

6. **Final Pay Email Quality**: Review the email to Catherine Moore for clear final pay coordination content, appropriate scope of information, and professional tone suitable for finance coordination.

7. **HRIS Update Quality**: Review Ryan O'Connor's HRIS changes for accuracy, completeness, and audit-appropriate language.

8. **Cross-Channel Consistency**: Spot-check names, dates, and details across all artifacts for consistency and absence of contradictions.

9. **Scope Discipline**: Review all state-changing actions for necessity — flag any extra emails, calendar events, chat messages to uninvolved parties, or unrelated system changes.
