## 1. PURPOSE AND PHILOSOPHY

Weaver Enterprises is committed to fostering a culture of continuous improvement, development, and achievement. Our performance management process is designed to:

- Align individual performance with organizational goals
- Provide regular feedback and coaching
- Recognize and reward strong performance
- Identify and address performance gaps
- Support employee development and career growth
- Make informed decisions about compensation, promotions, and succession planning

Performance management is an ongoing dialogue between managers and employees, not a once-a-year event. This policy establishes the framework for that ongoing conversation.

## 2. SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees. Temporary employees and interns follow a modified process as determined by their manager and HR.

**Probationary Period:**
- New hires are in a 90-day probationary period
- Performance evaluated at 30, 60, and 90 days
- Different evaluation process than annual review
- Successful completion required for continued employment

## 3. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT CYCLE

### 3.1 Annual Cycle Overview

The performance management cycle follows the calendar year:

| Activity | Timing | Participants |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| Goal Setting | January - February | Employee & Manager |
| Mid-Year Review | June - July | Employee & Manager |
| Self-Assessment | November | Employee |
| Annual Performance Review | December - January | Employee, Manager, & HR |
| Calibration | January | Leadership Team |
| Performance Ratings Finalized | January | Manager & HR |
| Compensation Decisions | February | Manager, HR, & Finance |

### 3.2 Continuous Feedback

In addition to formal reviews, managers should provide:
- Real-time feedback on specific situations
- Regular one-on-one meetings (at least bi-weekly)
- Informal check-ins on progress toward goals
- Recognition for achievements and milestones
- Constructive guidance when performance concerns arise

## 4. GOAL SETTING

### 4.1 SMART Goals Framework

All performance goals should be SMART:
- **S**pecific - Clear and unambiguous
- **M**easurable - Can track progress and results
- **A**chievable - Realistic given resources and constraints
- **R**elevant - Aligned with team and company objectives
- **T**ime-bound - Has defined timeline or deadline

### 4.2 Types of Goals

**Performance Goals:**
- What the employee will accomplish
- Specific deliverables and outcomes
- Key projects and initiatives
- Quality and productivity targets

**Development Goals:**
- Skills to build or enhance
- Learning and training activities
- Stretch assignments or new responsibilities
- Career development objectives

**Behavioral Goals:**
- Demonstration of company values
- Leadership competencies
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Communication and interpersonal skills

### 4.3 Goal Setting Process

**Employee Preparation:**
- Review company and department objectives
- Reflect on past performance and accomplishments
- Identify priorities for upcoming year
- Consider development areas and career aspirations
- Draft proposed goals

**Manager-Employee Discussion:**
- Review organizational priorities
- Discuss employee's proposed goals
- Ensure alignment with team objectives
- Confirm goals are SMART
- Agree on success criteria
- Document final goals in performance system

**Typical Number of Goals:**
- 3-5 performance goals
- 1-2 development goals
- All employees have behavioral expectations based on company values

### 4.4 Cascading Goals

Goals should cascade from organizational to individual level:
1. Company sets strategic objectives
2. Departments align goals to company strategy
3. Teams align goals to department objectives
4. Individuals align goals to team priorities

This ensures everyone's work contributes to organizational success.

### 4.5 Adjusting Goals Mid-Year

Goals may be adjusted when:
- Business priorities change significantly
- Role responsibilities change
- External circumstances require pivot
- Goals proven unrealistic or too easy

**Process for Adjusting Goals:**
- Manager and employee discuss need for change
- Document rationale for adjustment
- Update goals in performance system
- HR approval required for significant changes

## 5. MID-YEAR REVIEW

### 5.1 Purpose

The mid-year review is a formal checkpoint to:
- Assess progress toward annual goals
- Provide feedback on performance to date
- Identify any obstacles or support needed
- Adjust goals if necessary
- Discuss development and career aspirations

### 5.2 Mid-Year Review Process

**Employee Preparation:**
- Review goals set at beginning of year
- Assess progress on each goal
- Identify accomplishments and challenges
- Prepare questions or discussion topics

**Manager Preparation:**
- Review employee's goals and documentation
- Assess progress and performance
- Gather examples of performance (positive and developmental)
- Prepare feedback and coaching points

**Mid-Year Discussion:**
- Review progress on each goal
- Discuss what's working well
- Address challenges or obstacles
- Identify support or resources needed
- Adjust goals if necessary
- Discuss development progress

**Documentation:**
- Manager documents key discussion points
- Updates goal progress in system
- Notes any agreed-upon actions or changes

### 5.3 Mid-Year Ratings

Mid-year reviews are developmental, not evaluative:
- No formal performance rating assigned
- Focus on coaching and improvement
- Informal assessment of on-track vs. off-track
- Manager should be transparent about concerns

## 6. ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REVIEW

### 6.1 Self-Assessment

**Employee Responsibilities:**
- Complete self-assessment in November
- Evaluate performance against each goal
- Provide evidence of accomplishments
- Reflect on strengths and development areas
- Identify learning and growth during the year
- Suggest rating (optional)

**Self-Assessment Questions:**
- What were your key accomplishments this year?
- How did you progress on each goal?
- What challenges did you face and how did you address them?
- How did you demonstrate company values?
- What did you learn this year?
- What are your development priorities for next year?

### 6.2 Manager Assessment

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Review employee self-assessment
- Evaluate performance against goals and expectations
- Gather input from peers and stakeholders (360 feedback when applicable)
- Consider performance throughout entire year, not just recent months
- Document specific examples supporting rating
- Draft performance review comments

**Manager Assessment Considers:**
- Achievement of performance goals
- Quality of work and deliverables
- Demonstration of company values
- Growth and development
- Contribution to team success
- Initiative and problem-solving
- Collaboration and communication

### 6.3 Performance Review Discussion

**Scheduling:**
- Schedule 60-90 minutes in private setting
- Allow adequate time for discussion
- Avoid end of day or rushed timing
- Choose comfortable, confidential location

**Discussion Structure:**

1. **Opening (5 minutes)**
   - Set positive, constructive tone
   - Review purpose and format
   - Put employee at ease

2. **Employee Self-Reflection (15 minutes)**
   - Employee shares self-assessment
   - Discuss accomplishments and challenges
   - Manager listens actively, asks questions

3. **Manager Feedback (30 minutes)**
   - Review each goal and overall performance
   - Provide specific examples
   - Discuss strengths and areas for improvement
   - Share performance rating and rationale
   - Allow employee to respond and discuss

4. **Development Discussion (15 minutes)**
   - Discuss career aspirations
   - Identify development opportunities
   - Plan for skill-building
   - Discuss potential next roles or growth paths

5. **Looking Ahead (15 minutes)**
   - Preview next year's priorities
   - Discuss expectations and goals
   - Identify support needed
   - End on positive, forward-looking note

6. **Closing (5 minutes)**
   - Summarize key points
   - Confirm next steps
   - Allow for final questions
   - Thank employee for contributions

**Documentation:**
- Manager completes written review in system
- Employee can add comments or feedback
- Both manager and employee acknowledge review
- HR reviews for completeness and consistency

### 6.4 Calibration Process

**Purpose of Calibration:**
- Ensure consistency in ratings across organization
- Reduce bias and promote fairness
- Align on performance standards
- Validate proposed ratings before finalization

**Calibration Meetings:**
- Held at department or division level
- Led by department head with HR facilitation
- Managers discuss team members' performance
- Compare employees at similar levels
- Adjust ratings if inconsistencies identified

**Calibration Guidelines:**
- Focus on performance evidence, not opinions
- Consider full year, not recent events only
- Apply rating criteria consistently
- Challenge ratings that seem inflated or deflated
- Document rationale for any rating changes

## 7. PERFORMANCE RATINGS

### 7.1 Rating Scale

Weaver Enterprises uses a 5-point rating scale:

**5 - Exceptional:**
- Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas
- Demonstrates extraordinary impact
- Serves as role model for others
- Achieves exceptional results against challenging goals
- *Typical distribution: 5-10% of employees*

**4 - Exceeds Expectations:**
- Regularly exceeds expectations
- Achieves all goals and often goes beyond
- Strong demonstration of company values
- Makes significant contributions to team success
- *Typical distribution: 15-20% of employees*

**3 - Meets Expectations:**
- Consistently meets all performance expectations
- Achieves goals and objectives
- Demonstrates company values
- Reliable, solid contributor
- *Typical distribution: 60-70% of employees*

**2 - Partially Meets Expectations:**
- Performance meets some but not all expectations
- Some goals achieved, others not met
- Improvement needed in specific areas
- May be new to role or developing skills
- *Typical distribution: 5-10% of employees*

**1 - Does Not Meet Expectations:**
- Performance consistently below expectations
- Most goals not achieved
- Significant improvement required
- May result in performance improvement plan or termination
- *Typical distribution: Less than 5% of employees*

### 7.2 Rating Guidelines

**What Ratings Mean:**

A **"Meets Expectations" (3)** rating is a positive rating indicating:
- Employee is performing job successfully
- Meeting all core responsibilities
- Contributing to team effectively
- This is the expected and most common rating

**Exceeds Expectations (4) or Exceptional (5)** should be reserved for:
- Truly outstanding performance
- Going significantly beyond job requirements
- Delivering exceptional results
- Making substantial impact on organization

**Below Expectations (2 or 1)** indicates:
- Performance concerns requiring attention
- Goals not being met
- Need for improvement plan
- Potential risk to continued employment (for rating of 1)

### 7.3 Rating Distribution

While not enforced as strict quotas, expected rating distributions help ensure:
- Consistency across organization
- Meaningful differentiation
- Alignment with performance reality
- Fair compensation decisions

Managers should discuss with HR if their team's distribution significantly deviates from guidelines.

## 8. FEEDBACK AND COACHING

### 8.1 Effective Feedback

**Characteristics of Good Feedback:**
- Specific and concrete (not vague)
- Timely (close to the event)
- Focused on behavior, not personality
- Balanced (strengths and development areas)
- Actionable (includes suggestions for improvement)
- Two-way (allows for discussion)

**Positive Feedback:**
- Recognize specific accomplishments
- Explain impact of the behavior
- Encourage continued performance
- Provide publicly when appropriate

**Constructive Feedback:**
- Deliver privately
- Focus on specific behavior or outcome
- Explain impact or concern
- Discuss alternative approaches
- Create action plan for improvement
- Follow up on progress

### 8.2 SBI Feedback Model

Use the Situation-Behavior-Impact model:

**Situation:** Describe when and where
- "In yesterday's client meeting..."

**Behavior:** Describe what you observed
- "...you interrupted the client multiple times..."

**Impact:** Explain the effect
- "...which made them seem frustrated and cut the meeting short."

Then discuss and problem-solve together.

### 8.3 Regular One-on-Ones

Managers should hold regular one-on-one meetings with direct reports:

**Frequency:** At least bi-weekly (weekly preferred)

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes

**Employee-Driven Agenda:**
- Progress updates on goals and projects
- Obstacles or challenges
- Questions or support needed
- Ideas and suggestions
- Career development

**Manager's Role:**
- Listen actively
- Provide coaching and guidance
- Remove obstacles
- Offer feedback
- Discuss priorities

**Documentation:**
- Keep brief notes on key discussion points
- Track action items and commitments
- Note performance observations over time

## 9. PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS (PIP)

### 9.1 When PIPs Are Used

Performance Improvement Plans are formal interventions used when:
- Employee receives "Does Not Meet Expectations" rating
- Performance issues are serious or ongoing
- Previous coaching has not resulted in improvement
- Clear performance standards are not being met

PIPs are not appropriate for:
- Minor performance issues (use coaching)
- First-time mistakes or errors
- Short-term performance dips
- Misconduct (use disciplinary process instead)

### 9.2 PIP Process

**Step 1: Planning**
- Manager consults with HR before initiating PIP
- Review documentation of performance issues
- Ensure concerns are job-related and documented
- Determine if PIP is appropriate intervention

**Step 2: PIP Development**
- Identify specific performance deficiencies
- Set clear, measurable improvement goals
- Define timeline (typically 30-90 days)
- Specify support and resources provided
- Outline consequences if improvement not achieved

**Step 3: PIP Meeting**
- Manager meets with employee privately
- Review performance concerns with specific examples
- Present written PIP document
- Explain expectations and timeline
- Discuss support available
- Allow employee to ask questions
- Employee signs acknowledgment (signature = receipt, not agreement)

**Step 4: Monitoring and Support**
- Regular check-ins (weekly recommended)
- Provide ongoing feedback
- Document progress or lack thereof
- Adjust support if needed
- Keep HR informed

**Step 5: PIP Conclusion**
- Evaluate performance at end of PIP period
- Meet with employee to discuss results
- Possible outcomes:
  - **Successful:** Employee meets goals, PIP closed, return to normal performance management
  - **Partially Successful:** Extend PIP with revised goals
  - **Unsuccessful:** Performance still deficient, proceed to termination

### 9.3 PIP Documentation Requirements

**PIP Document Must Include:**
- Specific performance deficiencies with examples
- Clear performance standards expected
- Measurable goals for improvement
- Timeline and check-in schedule
- Support and resources provided
- Consequences for non-improvement
- Manager and employee signatures

**Ongoing Documentation:**
- Weekly check-in notes
- Specific examples of improvement or continued deficiency
- Any additional coaching or support provided
- Final evaluation at end of PIP period

### 9.4 Employee Rights During PIP

Employees on PIP:
- Have right to understand specific concerns
- Can ask questions and seek clarification
- May provide their perspective
- Should receive regular feedback during PIP
- Can request HR involvement or guidance
- May add comments to PIP documentation

## 10. COMPENSATION AND PERFORMANCE

### 10.1 Link to Compensation

Performance ratings influence compensation decisions:

**Merit Increases:**
- Annual merit increase budget allocated by performance rating
- Higher performers receive larger percentage increases
- Typical merit increase guidelines (when budget allows):
  - Exceptional (5): 4-6% increase
  - Exceeds Expectations (4): 3-4% increase
  - Meets Expectations (3): 2-3% increase
  - Partially Meets (2): 0-1% increase
  - Does Not Meet (1): No increase

**Bonuses:**
- Performance ratings determine bonus eligibility and amount
- Bonus targets vary by level and role
- Individual performance rating multiplied by bonus target
- Company performance also factors into bonus funding

### 10.2 Promotions

Promotions based on:
- Consistent strong performance (typically multiple years of Exceeds or Exceptional ratings)
- Demonstrated readiness for next level
- Operating at next level for sustained period
- Availability of role at next level
- Business need and budget

Promotion ≠ automatic. Strong performance is necessary but not sufficient for promotion.

### 10.3 Timing Considerations

**Compensation Review Cycle:**
- Performance reviews completed: December-January
- Calibration and finalization: January
- Compensation decisions: February
- Merit increases effective: March 1
- Bonuses paid: March (for prior year performance)

**Other Timing Factors:**
- Employees hired or promoted mid-year: prorated merit increase
- Employees on leave during review period: reviewed upon return
- Employees who change roles: reviewed by most recent manager with input from prior manager

- Formal training (courses, workshops, certifications)

- Mentoring and coaching

Managers should discuss career development at least annually:

- Manager's commitment to support growth

### 11.3 Succession Planning

Succession planning identifies and develops future leaders:
- High-potential employees identified
- Development plans created for critical roles
- Cross-training and knowledge transfer
- Leadership development programs
- Mentoring by senior leaders
