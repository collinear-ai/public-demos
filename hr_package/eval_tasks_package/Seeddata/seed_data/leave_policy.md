## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy outlines the various types of leave available to employees of Weaver Enterprises. The Company is committed to providing time off benefits that support employee well-being, work-life balance, and compliance with applicable federal and state laws.

This policy applies to all employees unless otherwise specified. Eligibility for specific leave types may vary based on employment status, length of service, and hours worked.

## 2. PAID TIME OFF (PTO)

### 2.1 Eligibility

All full-time employees are eligible for PTO. Part-time employees working 20+ hours per week accrue PTO on a prorated basis.

### 2.2 Accrual Rates

PTO accrues based on years of continuous service:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Accrual per Pay Period (bi-weekly) |
|------------------|-----------------|-------------------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.15 hours |
| 6-10 years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |
| 11+ years | 30 days (240 hours) | 9.23 hours |

**Accrual Details:**
- PTO begins accruing on the first day of employment
- Accrual is based on paid hours (does not accrue during unpaid leave)
- Part-time employees accrue PTO proportional to hours worked
- PTO accrual rates increase on the anniversary of hire date

### 2.3 PTO Usage

PTO may be used for:
- Vacation and personal time
- Medical appointments
- Family obligations
- Personal emergencies
- Any reason (no explanation required)

**Minimum Increments:**
- Full-day increments: 8 hours
- Half-day increments: 4 hours
- Hourly increments: 1 hour (for appointments or short absences)

### 2.4 Requesting PTO

**Planned PTO:**
- Submit requests through the timekeeping system at least 2 weeks in advance for 3+ consecutive days
- Submit at least 1 week in advance for 1-2 days
- Manager approval required before making travel arrangements
- Requests approved based on business needs and seniority (first-come, first-served when possible)

**Unplanned PTO:**
- Notify manager as soon as possible, ideally before start of shift
- Call directly or leave voicemail (email not sufficient for same-day notification)
- Provide expected return date
- Manager may require documentation for absences of 3+ consecutive days

**Blackout Periods:**
- Some departments may restrict PTO during peak business periods
- Employees notified at least 60 days in advance of blackout periods
- Emergency situations considered on case-by-case basis

### 2.5 PTO Carryover and Maximum Balance

**Carryover:**
- Employees may carry over up to 80 hours (10 days) of unused PTO to the next calendar year
- Any PTO exceeding 80 hours on December 31 is forfeited
- Employees notified in November if approaching maximum balance

**Maximum Accrual Cap:**
- PTO stops accruing once balance reaches 240 hours (30 days)
- Accrual resumes once balance falls below cap
- Employees encouraged to use PTO throughout the year

### 2.6 PTO Payout

**Upon Termination:**
- Employees who provide at least 2 weeks' notice: unused PTO paid out in final paycheck
- Employees terminated for cause or who quit without notice: forfeit unused PTO
- PTO payout calculated at employee's base rate of pay at time of separation

**No Cash-Out While Employed:**
- PTO cannot be cashed out during active employment
- Exception: Company-approved buyback programs (if offered)

### 2.7 PTO During Notice Period

Employees who resign must use PTO during their notice period only with manager approval. The Company reserves the right to pay out PTO in lieu of time off during the notice period.

## 3. SICK LEAVE

### 3.1 Eligibility and Accrual

All employees accrue sick leave separate from PTO:
- Accrual rate: 1 hour of sick leave for every 30 hours worked
- Maximum annual accrual: 40 hours (5 days)
- Begins accruing on first day of employment
- Unused sick leave carries over year to year (up to 80 hours maximum balance)

### 3.2 Permitted Uses of Sick Leave

Sick leave may be used for:
- Employee's own illness, injury, or medical condition
- Medical, dental, or vision appointments
- Care for family member with illness or medical condition
- Absences due to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking
- Public health emergency closure or quarantine

**Eligible Family Members:**
- Spouse or domestic partner
- Children (biological, adopted, step, or foster)
- Parents (biological, adoptive, step, or foster)
- Grandparents and grandchildren
- Siblings

### 3.3 Requesting Sick Leave

**Notification Requirements:**
- Notify manager before scheduled shift start time
- Provide expected duration of absence
- Update manager if absence will be longer than initially anticipated

**Documentation:**
- Medical documentation may be required for absences of 3+ consecutive days
- Documentation must include: dates of absence, reason (illness/injury/medical appointment), and healthcare provider signature
- Submit documentation within 5 business days of return to work

### 3.4 Sick Leave Payout

- Sick leave is NOT paid out upon termination
- Sick leave cannot be cashed out during employment
- Accrued sick leave is forfeited upon separation from Company

## 4. COMPANY HOLIDAYS

### 4.1 Observed Holidays

Weaver Enterprises observes the following paid holidays:

1. New Year's Day (January 1)
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (third Monday in January)
3. Presidents' Day (third Monday in February)
4. Memorial Day (last Monday in May)
5. Juneteenth (June 19)
6. Independence Day (July 4)
7. Labor Day (first Monday in September)
8. Thanksgiving Day (fourth Thursday in November)
9. Day After Thanksgiving
10. Christmas Day (December 25)

**Floating Holidays:**
- 2 additional floating holidays per year
- Must be scheduled in advance with manager approval
- Use by December 31 or forfeit (no carryover)

### 4.2 Holiday Eligibility

- Full-time employees receive full holiday pay (8 hours)
- Part-time employees receive prorated holiday pay based on regular schedule
- Must be an active employee on the holiday to receive holiday pay

**Holiday Pay When Not Scheduled to Work:**
- Employees normally scheduled to work on the holiday receive holiday pay
- Employees not normally scheduled to work that day of week do not receive additional pay

### 4.3 Holidays Falling on Weekends

- Holiday falls on Saturday: observed on preceding Friday
- Holiday falls on Sunday: observed on following Monday

### 4.4 Working on Holidays

Non-exempt employees required to work on an observed holiday receive:
- Regular holiday pay (8 hours) PLUS
- Time-and-a-half pay for all hours actually worked

Exempt employees receive a floating holiday to use at a later date if required to work on an observed holiday.

## 5. FAMILY AND MEDICAL LEAVE (FMLA)

### 5.1 Eligibility Requirements

Employees are eligible for FMLA leave if they:
- Have worked for Weaver Enterprises for at least 12 months (need not be consecutive)
- Have worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12 months prior to leave
- Work at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### 5.2 Qualifying Reasons for FMLA Leave

FMLA leave may be taken for:
- Birth of a child and to care for the newborn (within 1 year of birth)
- Placement of a child for adoption or foster care (within 1 year of placement)
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with a serious health condition
- Employee's own serious health condition that prevents work performance
- Qualifying exigency arising from family member's military service
- Care for covered service member with serious injury or illness (up to 26 weeks)

**Serious Health Condition Defined:**
- Illness, injury, impairment, or physical/mental condition requiring:
  - Inpatient care, OR
  - Continuing treatment by a healthcare provider

### 5.3 Amount of FMLA Leave

**Standard FMLA:**
- Up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave within a 12-month rolling period
- Rolling 12-month period measured backward from date of FMLA use

**Military Caregiver Leave:**
- Up to 26 weeks in a single 12-month period to care for covered service member

**Intermittent or Reduced Schedule Leave:**
- Leave may be taken in smaller increments when medically necessary
- Must be certified by healthcare provider
- Minimum increment: 1 hour

### 5.4 Request and Certification Process

**Employee Responsibilities:**

**For Foreseeable Leave:**
- Provide 30 days' advance notice when leave is foreseeable
- Contact HR to request FMLA and complete request form
- Submit medical certification within 15 days

**For Unforeseeable Leave:**
- Notify manager and HR as soon as possible (within 1-2 business days)
- Complete FMLA request form within 5 business days of leave start
- Submit medical certification within 15 days

**Medical Certification Requirements:**
- Healthcare provider must complete Company's certification form
- Must include: diagnosis, treatment plan, expected duration, and work limitations
- Recertification may be required every 6 months or as circumstances change
- Company may request second opinion at Company expense

### 5.5 Pay During FMLA Leave

FMLA leave is unpaid. However, employees must use accrued paid leave concurrently:

**Leave Substitution Order:**
1. Sick leave (if qualifying medical reason)
2. PTO
3. Unpaid FMLA leave (after paid leave exhausted)

Employees may not reserve PTO to use after FMLA leave is exhausted.

### 5.6 Benefits During FMLA Leave

**Health Insurance:**
- Company continues health insurance on same terms as if employee were actively working
- Employee must continue paying their share of premiums
- If employee fails to pay premiums, coverage may be cancelled after 30-day grace period

**Other Benefits:**
- PTO and sick leave do not accrue during unpaid FMLA leave
- Seniority and employment benefits accrued before leave are retained
- 401(k) contributions pause during unpaid leave

### 5.7 Return from FMLA Leave

**Job Restoration:**
- Employee returned to same position or equivalent position with:
  - Same pay and benefits
  - Equivalent duties and responsibilities
  - Same shift and location (or similar)

**Key Employee Exception:**
- Highest-paid 10% of employees may be denied job restoration if it would cause substantial economic injury to Company
- Advance notice provided if this applies

**Fitness for Duty:**
- Employee must provide fitness-for-duty certification before returning from medical leave
- Certification must address ability to perform essential job functions
- May not delay return if certification submitted timely

### 5.8 Failure to Return from FMLA Leave

If an employee fails to return from FMLA leave:
- Employment may be terminated
- Company may recover health insurance premiums paid during leave (unless failure to return due to circumstances beyond employee's control)

## 6. PARENTAL LEAVE

### 6.1 Paid Parental Leave

In addition to FMLA, Weaver Enterprises provides paid parental leave:

**Eligibility:**
- All full-time employees
- No waiting period (available from first day of employment)

**Amount:**
- 8 weeks (320 hours) of paid leave at 100% of base salary
- Available for birth, adoption, or foster care placement
- Must be used within 12 months of child's birth or placement

**Coordination with FMLA:**
- Paid parental leave runs concurrently with FMLA (if eligible)
- After 8 weeks of paid leave, remaining FMLA is unpaid (or may use accrued PTO/sick leave)

**Usage:**
- May be taken continuously or intermittently with manager approval
- Intermittent leave subject to business needs
- Both parents employed by Company may each take 8 weeks

### 6.2 Requesting Parental Leave

**Advance Notice:**
- Provide at least 30 days' notice for planned leave (birth, adoption)
- For unplanned situations, notify HR as soon as possible

**Documentation:**
- Submit birth certificate, adoption papers, or foster placement documents within 30 days

## 7. BEREAVEMENT LEAVE

### 7.1 Paid Bereavement Leave

Employees receive paid time off following the death of a family member:

**Amount of Leave:**
- Up to 5 days for immediate family member
- Up to 3 days for extended family member

**Immediate Family:**
- Spouse or domestic partner
- Children (biological, adopted, step, foster)
- Parents (biological, adoptive, step, foster)
- Siblings
- Grandparents, grandchildren

**Extended Family:**
- Aunts, uncles, cousins
- Nieces, nephews
- In-laws (parents, siblings)

### 7.2 Requesting Bereavement Leave

- Notify manager as soon as possible
- Contact HR to report absence and expected duration
- Provide obituary, death certificate, or funeral program within 5 business days of return

**Additional Time:**
- If more time is needed, employee may use PTO or request unpaid leave
- FMLA may be available if death causes serious health condition for employee

## 8. JURY DUTY AND WITNESS LEAVE

### 8.1 Jury Duty Leave

**Pay:**
- Full-time employees receive regular pay for up to 10 days of jury duty service
- Employees must remit any jury duty pay received from court to the Company (excluding mileage reimbursement)

**Notification:**
- Provide copy of jury summons to manager and HR as soon as received
- Report to work if excused from jury duty for any portion of workday

### 8.2 Witness or Subpoena Leave

Employees subpoenaed to appear in court receive unpaid leave. Employees may use PTO to receive pay during this absence.

**Exception:** If testifying on behalf of the Company or at Company's request, time is paid and considered time worked.

## 9. MILITARY LEAVE

### 9.1 Uniformed Services Leave

Employees who serve in the uniformed services are entitled to unpaid military leave in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).

**Covered Service:**
- Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Coast Guard
- Army National Guard and Air National Guard
- Commissioned Corps of the Public Health Service
- Reserve components of these services

**Duration:**
- Up to 5 years of cumulative service (with exceptions for certain situations)

### 9.2 Employee Responsibilities

- Provide advance notice of military service (verbal or written)
- Provide copy of military orders when available
- Notify Company of intent to return to work within required timeframe after service completion

### 9.3 Reemployment Rights

Employees who satisfactorily complete military service are entitled to:
- Reemployment in the position they would have held if continuously employed (escalator position)
- Seniority and benefits as if continuously employed
- Timely reinstatement based on length of service:
  - Less than 31 days: report by next scheduled work period
  - 31-180 days: apply within 14 days of completion
  - 181+ days: apply within 90 days of completion

### 9.4 Benefits During Military Leave

- Health insurance may be continued for up to 24 months (employee pays full premium plus 2%)
- PTO and sick leave do not accrue during unpaid military leave
- Service time counts toward vesting and benefit accrual upon return

## 10. VOTING LEAVE

Employees may take up to 2 hours of paid time off to vote in federal, state, or local elections if they do not have sufficient time outside of working hours.

**Requirements:**
- Provide at least 2 days' advance notice to manager
- Coordinate with manager on timing (typically at start or end of shift)
- Proof of voting may be required (such as "I Voted" sticker or other verification)

## 11. UNPAID PERSONAL LEAVE OF ABSENCE

### 11.1 Eligibility and Request Process

Employees may request unpaid personal leave for reasons not covered by other leave types.

**Eligibility:**
- Full-time employees with at least 1 year of service
- Must have exhausted all paid leave (PTO, sick leave)
- Leave granted at Company discretion based on business needs

**Request Process:**
- Submit written request to HR at least 30 days in advance (when possible)
- Include: reason for leave, requested start date, expected duration
- Manager and HR must approve request
- Maximum duration: 30 days (extensions may be granted in exceptional circumstances)

### 11.2 Benefits During Unpaid Personal Leave

- Health insurance may be continued at employee's expense (full premium plus 2%)
- PTO and sick leave do not accrue
- Seniority continues to accrue
- Employment benefits earned before leave are retained

### 11.3 Return from Personal Leave

- Employee must notify HR of return date at least 1 week in advance
- Company will make reasonable efforts to return employee to same or similar position
- No job restoration guarantee for personal leave (unlike FMLA)

## 12. LEAVE ADMINISTRATION

### 12.1 General Guidelines

**Requesting Leave:**
- Use Company's leave management system for all leave requests
- Contact HR Leave Administration team for questions: leaves@weaverenterprises.com or (555) 123-4575

**Medical Documentation:**
- May be required for absences of 3+ consecutive days
- Must be from treating healthcare provider
- Submit within required timeframe or leave may be denied

**Confidentiality:**
- Medical information kept confidential and stored separately from personnel file
- Shared only with individuals who have legitimate business need to know

### 12.2 Coordination of Leaves

When multiple leave types apply, they run concurrently:
- FMLA + Paid Parental Leave = 12 weeks total (8 weeks paid + 4 weeks unpaid)
- State disability + FMLA = Leaves run concurrently
- Workers' compensation + FMLA = May run concurrently if FMLA-qualifying

### 12.3 State-Specific Leave Laws

Some states have additional leave requirements beyond federal law. Employees working in states with additional requirements will receive supplemental state-specific information.

**States with Additional Requirements:**
- California (California Family Rights Act, Paid Family Leave)
- New York (New York Paid Family Leave)
- Washington (Washington Paid Family and Medical Leave)
- Massachusetts (Massachusetts Paid Family and Medical Leave)
- And others as applicable

Employees should contact HR to understand state-specific entitlements.

## 13. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 13.1 Employee Responsibilities

- Provide timely notice of need for leave
- Submit required documentation within specified timeframes
- Keep manager and HR informed of leave status
- Maintain contact during extended leave
- Provide return-to-work date and fitness-for-duty certification (when applicable)
- Continue benefit premium payments during leave

### 13.2 Manager Responsibilities

- Accept and forward leave requests to HR immediately
- Do not approve or deny FMLA-qualifying leave (HR handles FMLA determinations)
- Maintain confidentiality of employee medical information
- Facilitate smooth transition before and after leave
- Do not retaliate against employees for taking protected leave
- Contact HR with questions about leave administration

### 13.3 HR Responsibilities

- Determine leave eligibility
- Provide required notices and forms
- Track leave usage and duration
- Coordinate benefits during leave
- Process medical certifications
- Ensure compliance with federal and state leave laws
- Facilitate return to work
