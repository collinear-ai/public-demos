## 2. IMPORTANT PLAN DETAILS

### 2.1 Overall Deductible

**Network Providers:**
- Individual: $2,550 per calendar year
- Family: $5,100 per calendar year

**Out-of-Network Providers:**
- Individual: $5,100 per calendar year
- Family: $10,200 per calendar year

**How It Works:** Generally, you must pay all of the costs from providers up to the deductible amount before this plan begins to pay. If you have other family members on the plan, each family member must meet their own individual deductible until the total amount of deductible expenses paid by all family members meets the overall family deductible.

### 2.2 Services Covered Before You Meet Your Deductible

Yes. The following services are covered before you meet your deductible:

- Preventive care
- Primary care visits
- Urgent care
- Specialist visits
- Outpatient services for behavioral health
- Office visits
- Rehabilitation services
- Habilitation services
- Children's eye exams
- Children's dental check-up

**Note:** This plan covers some items and services even if you haven't yet met the deductible amount. But a copayment or coinsurance may apply. For example, this plan covers certain preventive services without cost sharing and before you meet your deductible. See a list of covered preventive services at www.healthcare.gov/coverage/preventive-care-benefits/.

### 2.3 Other Deductibles for Specific Services

**Prescription Drugs:**
- Individual: $350 per calendar year
- Family: $700 per calendar year
- **Exception:** Does not apply to Tier 1 drugs

**Other Deductibles:** There are no other specific deductibles.

**How It Works:** You must pay all of the costs for these services up to the specific deductible amount before this plan begins to pay for these services.

### 2.4 Out-of-Pocket Limit

**Network Providers:**
- Individual: $9,200 per calendar year
- Family: $18,400 per calendar year

**Out-of-Network Providers:**
- Individual: $18,400 per calendar year
- Family: $36,800 per calendar year

**How It Works:** The out-of-pocket limit is the most you could pay in a year for covered services. If you have other family members in this plan, they have to meet their own out-of-pocket limits until the overall family out-of-pocket limit has been met.

### 2.5 What Is Not Included in the Out-of-Pocket Limit

The following expenses do NOT count toward the out-of-pocket limit:

- Premiums
- Balance-billing charges (unless balance billing is prohibited)
- Health care this plan doesn't cover
- Penalties for failure to obtain preauthorization for services

Even though you pay these expenses, they don't count toward the out-of-pocket limit.

### 2.6 Network Provider Benefits

**Will you pay less if you use a network provider?** Yes.

**Network Information:** See www.welcometouhc.com or call 1-800-782-3740 for a list of network providers.

**Important Notes:**
- This plan uses a provider network. You will pay less if you use a provider in the plan's network.
- You will pay the most if you use an out-of-network provider, and you might receive a bill from a provider for the difference between the provider's charge and what your plan pays (balance billing).
- Be aware, your network provider might use an out-of-network provider for some services (such as lab work). Check with your provider before you get services.

### 2.7 Specialist Referral Requirements

**Do you need a referral to see a specialist?** No.

You can see the specialist you choose without a referral.

## 4. NETWORK PROVIDER INFORMATION

### 4.1 Finding Network Providers

To find network providers:
- Visit: www.welcometouhc.com
- Call: 1-800-782-3740

### 4.2 Network vs Out-of-Network Costs

**Network Providers:**
- Lower out-of-pocket costs
- Predictable copays and coinsurance
- No balance billing (in most cases)

**Out-of-Network Providers:**
- Higher out-of-pocket costs
- Higher coinsurance percentages
- May be subject to balance billing
- Some services may not be covered

### 4.3 Important Reminders

- Always check if your provider is in-network before receiving services
- Even if your primary provider is in-network, they may use out-of-network providers for certain services (such as lab work or specialists)
- Check with your provider before receiving services to confirm network status
- Emergency services are covered at the same rate whether in-network or out-of-network
