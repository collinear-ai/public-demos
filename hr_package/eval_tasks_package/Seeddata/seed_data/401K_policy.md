## 1. PLAN OVERVIEW

The Weaver Enterprises Inc. 401(k) Retirement Savings Plan ("the Plan") is designed to help employees save for retirement through tax-advantaged contributions and Company matching contributions. The Plan is a defined contribution plan qualified under Section 401(k) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### 1.1 Plan Benefits

- Pre-tax and Roth after-tax contribution options
- Employer matching contributions
- Immediate vesting of Company match
- Wide variety of investment options
- Tax-deferred growth (or tax-free for Roth)
- Portable - take your account with you if you leave
- Loan and hardship withdrawal provisions
- Professional investment advice available

### 1.2 Plan Administrator

**Fidelity Investments** serves as the Plan's recordkeeper and trustee.

**Contact Information:**
- Website: www.401k.com
- Phone: 1-800-555-0401
- Mobile App: Fidelity NetBenefits

## 2. ELIGIBILITY AND ENROLLMENT

### 2.1 Eligibility Requirements

**Eligible Employees:**
- All employees age 21 and older
- Must complete 90 days of service
- Eligibility begins on the first day of the month following completion of 90-day waiting period

**Excluded Employees:**
- Independent contractors
- Leased employees
- Non-resident aliens with no U.S.-source income
- Union employees (unless union agreement provides for participation)

### 2.2 Automatic Enrollment

**Automatic Enrollment Feature:**
- All eligible employees are automatically enrolled at 3% of eligible compensation
- Enrollment occurs on eligibility date unless employee actively opts out
- Contributions directed to Plan's default investment option (target-date fund based on age)
- Employees may change contribution rate or opt out at any time

**Automatic Increase Program (Optional):**
- Employees may elect to automatically increase contribution by 1% each year
- Increases occur each January 1st
- Continues until reaching 10% or employee-specified maximum
- Employees may opt in or out at any time

### 2.3 How to Enroll or Make Changes

**Online Enrollment:**
1. Visit www.401k.com
2. Log in using Social Security Number and PIN (provided after eligibility)
3. Select contribution rate (1%-100% of eligible pay)
4. Choose contribution type (pre-tax, Roth, or both)
5. Select investments or use professionally managed option
6. Designate beneficiaries

**Phone Enrollment:**
- Call Fidelity at 1-800-555-0401
- Representatives available Monday-Friday, 8am-10pm ET

**Changes Effective:**
- Enrollment or changes submitted by 4pm ET take effect within 1-2 pay periods

## 3. CONTRIBUTIONS

### 3.1 Employee Contributions

**Contribution Types:**

**Pre-Tax Contributions:**
- Deducted from pay before income taxes
- Reduces current taxable income
- Taxes paid upon withdrawal in retirement
- Growth is tax-deferred

**Roth After-Tax Contributions:**
- Deducted from pay after income taxes
- Does not reduce current taxable income
- Qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement (including earnings)
- Must meet 5-year rule and be age 59½ for tax-free withdrawal

Employees may contribute pre-tax, Roth, or a combination of both.

### 3.2 Contribution Limits

**2024 IRS Limits:**
- Elective deferral limit: $23,000 per year
- Catch-up contributions (age 50+): Additional $7,500 per year
- Total catch-up limit for age 50+: $30,500 per year

**Total Contribution Limit (employee + employer):**
- $69,000 per year (or $76,500 with catch-up for age 50+)

**Highly Compensated Employee Limits:**
- Employees earning $155,000+ may be subject to additional IRS-imposed limits
- Notifications sent if limits affect your contributions

### 3.3 Eligible Compensation

Contributions are calculated as a percentage of eligible compensation:

**Includes:**
- Base salary or hourly wages
- Overtime pay
- Bonuses and commissions
- Shift differentials

**Excludes:**
- Reimbursements and expense allowances
- Employer contributions to benefit plans
- Severance pay
- Equity compensation (stock options, RSUs)

**Annual Compensation Limit:**
- IRS limits eligible compensation to $345,000 for 2024

### 3.4 Changing Your Contributions

- Change contribution percentage anytime online or by phone
- Increase, decrease, or stop contributions at any time
- Start contributions again at any time if previously opted out
- Changes take effect within 1-2 pay periods

## 4. EMPLOYER MATCHING CONTRIBUTIONS

### 4.1 Company Match Formula

Weaver Enterprises provides a generous matching contribution:

**Match Structure:**
- 100% match on the first 3% of eligible compensation contributed
- 50% match on the next 2% of eligible compensation contributed

**Examples:**

| Employee Contributes | Company Match | Total Contribution |
|---------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1% | 1% | 2% |
| 3% | 3% | 6% |
| 5% | 4% (3% + 0.5×2%) | 9% |
| 6%+ | 4% (maximum match) | 10%+ |

To receive the maximum Company match of 4%, employees must contribute at least 5% of eligible compensation.

### 4.2 Match Timing

- Company match contributions deposited each pay period
- Match based on per-pay-period contributions (not annual total)
- To maximize match, contribute consistently throughout the year

### 4.3 Match Eligibility

- All participating employees receive match (no waiting period)
- Must be employed on the date contributions are made to receive match for that pay period
- No match on contributions made after separation from Company

### 4.4 True-Up Contribution

The Plan does not include an annual true-up contribution. To maximize the Company match, avoid front-loading contributions early in the year if you will reach the annual limit before year-end.

## 8. ACCESSING YOUR MONEY

### 8.1 While Employed

**In-Service Withdrawals:**

Generally, you cannot withdraw funds while actively employed, except:

**Age 59½ Withdrawals:**
- Available once you reach age 59½
- No penalty or special requirements
- Taxes apply (unless Roth qualified withdrawals)

**Hardship Withdrawals:**
- Available for immediate and heavy financial need
- Limited to employee contributions only (not earnings or match)
- Must demonstrate financial hardship
- Subject to taxes and 10% early withdrawal penalty (if under age 59½)
- See Section 8.3 for qualifying hardships

**Loans:**
- Borrow from your own account
- Must be repaid with interest
- See Section 8.4 for loan provisions

### 8.2 After Separation from Service

**Distribution Options After Leaving Company:**

**Option 1: Leave Money in Plan**
- Keep account in Plan if balance exceeds $7,000
- Continue to manage investments
- No additional contributions allowed
- Take distributions later when needed

**Option 2: Rollover to IRA or New Employer's Plan**
- Roll over to traditional IRA (pre-tax funds)
- Roll over to Roth IRA (Roth funds, or convert pre-tax funds with taxes)
- Roll over to new employer's 401(k) plan (if accepted)
- Maintains tax-deferred/tax-free status
- No taxes or penalties

**Option 3: Cash Distribution**
- Receive lump-sum payment
- Subject to income taxes
- 10% early withdrawal penalty if under age 59½ (with exceptions)
- 20% mandatory federal withholding

**Automatic Cashout:**
- If balance is $1,000-$7,000 and you don't make an election, automatically rolled to IRA
- If balance is under $1,000, automatically cashed out and check mailed

### 8.3 Hardship Withdrawals

**Qualifying Hardship Reasons:**
- Medical expenses for employee, spouse, or dependents
- Costs related to purchase of primary residence (excluding mortgage payments)
- Tuition and educational fees for next 12 months
- Payments to prevent eviction or foreclosure on primary residence
- Burial or funeral expenses for family members
- Expenses to repair damage to primary residence (casualty loss)
- Expenses and losses due to federally declared disaster

**Requirements:**
- Must demonstrate immediate and heavy financial need
- Must have exhausted other resources (loans, insurance, other assets)
- Amount limited to need (plus taxes and penalties)
- Cannot contribute to Plan for 6 months after hardship withdrawal

**Application Process:**
- Complete hardship withdrawal request form
- Provide supporting documentation
- HR and plan administrator review and approve
- Processing time: 7-10 business days

### 8.4 Loans

**Loan Provisions:**
- Borrow minimum of $1,000
- Borrow maximum of lesser of:
  - $50,000 (reduced by highest outstanding loan balance in past 12 months), OR
  - 50% of vested account balance
- Maximum of 2 loans outstanding at any time

**Loan Terms:**
- General purpose loan: Up to 5-year repayment
- Primary residence loan: Up to 15-year repayment
- Interest rate: Prime rate + 1% (fixed at loan origination)
- Interest paid back to your own account

**Repayment:**
- Automatic payroll deduction
- Payments made at least quarterly
- Level amortization (equal payments)

**Fees:**
- Loan origination fee: $75 (one-time)
- Annual maintenance fee: $50 per loan
- Fees deducted from loan proceeds or account balance

**Defaulted Loans:**
- Loan becomes taxable distribution if not repaid
- 10% early withdrawal penalty applies if under age 59½
- Loan defaults if:
  - Payments not made for 90+ days
  - Employment terminates (loan due within 60 days)

**Application:**
- Apply online at www.401k.com or call 1-800-555-0401
- Funds typically available within 5 business days
- Spouse consent required for loans over $5,000

### 8.5 Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

**When RMDs Begin:**
- Age 73 (or 75 if born in 1960 or later)
- Must begin by April 1 following year you turn required age
- Exception: Can delay if still employed (and not 5% owner)

**How RMDs Are Calculated:**
- Based on IRS life expectancy tables
- Calculated each year based on prior year-end balance
- Fidelity calculates and notifies you of RMD amount

**Tax Treatment:**
- Pre-tax account RMDs are taxable income
- Roth account RMDs are tax-free (if qualified)
- Failure to take RMD results in 25% penalty on amount not taken

## 9. BENEFICIARY DESIGNATIONS

### 9.1 Importance of Beneficiary Designation

- Designate who receives your account balance if you die
- Beneficiaries avoid probate process
- Can designate primary and contingent beneficiaries
- Can split percentage among multiple beneficiaries

### 9.2 Spousal Rights

**If You Are Married:**
- Spouse is automatic beneficiary unless spouse signs written consent to name someone else
- Spousal consent must be notarized
- Applies even if you designated someone else before marriage

**If You Are Not Married:**
- Can name anyone as beneficiary (person, trust, charity, estate)
- No consent required

### 9.3 Updating Beneficiaries

- Review and update beneficiary designations regularly
- Update after major life events: marriage, divorce, births, deaths
- Update online at www.401k.com or by submitting paper form
- Previous designation revoked by new designation

### 9.4 If No Beneficiary Designated

If no beneficiary is designated or all beneficiaries predecease you:
1. Spouse (if married)
2. Children (equally)
3. Parents (equally)
4. Estate

## 11. SPECIAL SITUATIONS

### 11.1 Military Leave

**During Military Service:**
- Can continue contributions if receiving pay
- Employer match continues on contributions made
- Can contribute missed deferrals when returning (within specified timeframe)

**Returning from Military Service:**
- Treated as continuously employed for vesting purposes
- Eligible for makeup contributions based on military pay

### 11.2 Leave of Absence

**During Approved Unpaid Leave:**
- Cannot make contributions (no compensation)
- No employer match while not receiving pay
- Account remains invested
- Can make investment changes
- Vesting service may continue depending on leave type (FMLA, military)

### 11.3 Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDRO)

If you divorce, a court may order that part of your account be paid to your former spouse:
- Order must be a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO)
- Company reviews order to ensure it meets Plan and legal requirements
- Once approved, account divided according to order
- Former spouse (alternate payee) can roll over or take distribution

**QDRO Review Fee:** $500 (charged to participant account)

### 11.4 Disability

If you become disabled and receive long-term disability benefits:
- Can continue contributions if receiving disability pay from Company
- If disability pay ceases, contributions stop
- Account remains invested
- May access funds if disabled (before age 59½) without 10% penalty

### 11.5 Death

**Upon Participant Death:**
- Beneficiaries notified and sent claim forms
- Account balance paid to designated beneficiaries
- Spousal beneficiaries have multiple options (keep in plan, rollover, distribute)
- Non-spousal beneficiaries must take distributions (but can stretch over lifetime)

## 13. TAX INFORMATION

### 13.1 Tax Treatment of Contributions

**Pre-Tax Contributions:**
- Reduce taxable income in year contributed
- Reported in Box 12 of W-2 with code D
- Reduce federal income tax (and usually state)
- Do not reduce Social Security or Medicare tax

**Roth Contributions:**
- Made with after-tax dollars
- Do not reduce taxable income
- Reported in Box 12 of W-2 with code AA

### 13.2 Tax Treatment of Distributions

**Pre-Tax Account Distributions:**
- Fully taxable as ordinary income
- Added to taxable income in year received
- 10% early withdrawal penalty if under age 59½ (with exceptions)

**Roth Account Distributions:**
- Contributions always tax-free
- Earnings tax-free if qualified distribution (age 59½ + 5-year rule)
- Earnings taxable if non-qualified distribution
- 10% penalty applies to taxable portion if under age 59½

**Withholding:**
- 20% mandatory federal withholding on eligible rollover distributions paid to you
- Can avoid withholding by choosing direct rollover
- State withholding may also apply

### 13.3 Form 1099-R

- Fidelity issues Form 1099-R by January 31 for prior year distributions
- Reports taxable distributions to IRS
- Use to prepare tax return
- Keep for your records

### 13.4 Consult a Tax Advisor

This summary provides general tax information. Individual tax situations vary. Consult a qualified tax advisor for advice specific to your situation.
