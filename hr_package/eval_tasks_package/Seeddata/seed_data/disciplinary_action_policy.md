## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes guidelines for addressing employee misconduct and policy violations at Weaver Enterprises. The purpose is to:

- Correct unacceptable behavior and performance issues
- Ensure fair and consistent treatment of all employees
- Maintain a productive and professional work environment
- Comply with employment laws and regulations
- Provide employees with clear expectations and opportunities to improve
- Protect the organization and other employees

This policy applies to all employees. The Company reserves the right to exercise discretion in determining appropriate disciplinary action based on the specific circumstances of each situation.

## 2. EMPLOYMENT AT-WILL

Employment at Weaver Enterprises is at-will, meaning either the employee or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice.

**This policy does not:**
- Create a contract of employment
- Guarantee employment for any specific period
- Require progressive discipline before termination
- Limit the Company's right to terminate employment

Progressive discipline is generally followed when appropriate, but the Company reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination for serious violations.

## 3. GENERAL PRINCIPLES

### 3.1 Consistency and Fairness

- Similar violations should result in similar consequences
- Discipline should be proportionate to the severity of the offense
- Individual circumstances may warrant different approaches
- Documentation is essential for all disciplinary actions

### 3.2 Corrective Purpose

The goal of discipline is correction, not punishment:
- Help employees understand expectations
- Provide opportunity to improve
- Support employees in meeting standards
- Separate employment only when necessary

### 3.3 Confidentiality

- Disciplinary matters kept confidential
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- Employee privacy respected
- Documentation stored securely in personnel files

### 3.4 Non-Retaliation

Employees will not face retaliation for:
- Reporting policy violations in good faith
- Participating in investigations
- Exercising legal rights
- Engaging in protected activities

Retaliation is itself a serious policy violation subject to discipline.

## 4. PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 Progressive Discipline Steps

When appropriate, the Company follows progressive discipline:

**Step 1: Verbal Warning**
- Informal discussion of concern
- Explanation of expected behavior
- Documentation in manager's notes
- No formal documentation in personnel file

**Step 2: Written Warning**
- Formal written documentation
- Specific description of violation
- Expected corrective action
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences of continued violations
- Placed in personnel file

**Step 3: Final Written Warning**
- Formal documentation of continued or serious violation
- Clear statement that employment is in jeopardy
- Specific expectations and timeline
- Notification that next violation may result in termination
- Placed in personnel file

**Step 4: Suspension (with or without pay)**
- Employee removed from workplace temporarily
- Allows for investigation or cooling-off period
- May be paid or unpaid depending on circumstances
- Duration typically 1-5 days
- Return-to-work conditions specified

**Step 5: Termination**
- Employment relationship ended
- Final step in progressive discipline
- Or immediate action for serious violations

### 4.2 When Progressive Discipline Applies

Progressive discipline is generally appropriate for:
- Attendance and punctuality issues
- Minor policy violations
- Work quality or productivity concerns (when not addressed through performance management)
- First-time minor infractions
- Situations where employee can reasonably improve

### 4.3 When Progressive Discipline May Be Skipped

The Company may bypass progressive discipline and proceed directly to suspension or termination for:
- Serious misconduct or policy violations
- Illegal activity
- Behavior that threatens safety or security
- Gross insubordination
- Theft, fraud, or dishonesty
- Violence or threats of violence
- Harassment or discrimination
- Breach of confidentiality
- Intoxication or drug use at work
- Willful destruction of Company property
- Conduct causing significant harm to Company or employees

## 5. COMMON VIOLATIONS AND POTENTIAL CONSEQUENCES

### 5.1 Attendance and Punctuality

**Examples:**
- Excessive absenteeism
- Chronic tardiness
- Leaving work early without permission
- No-call, no-show
- Abuse of break or lunch periods

**Typical Discipline:**
- 1st occurrence: Verbal warning
- 2nd occurrence: Written warning
- 3rd occurrence: Final written warning
- 4th occurrence: Termination

**Exception:** No-call, no-show for 3 consecutive days may result in immediate termination for job abandonment.

### 5.2 Performance Issues

**Examples:**
- Failure to meet job requirements
- Substandard work quality
- Lack of productivity
- Failure to follow instructions

**Note:** Performance issues are generally addressed through performance management process (see Performance Management Policy), not disciplinary action. Discipline appropriate when performance issues involve:
- Willful failure to perform duties
- Refusal to follow reasonable instructions
- Negligent or careless work causing harm

### 5.3 Policy Violations

**Examples:**
- Dress code violations
- Inappropriate use of technology
- Smoking in prohibited areas
- Failure to follow safety procedures
- Unauthorized overtime

**Typical Discipline:**
- 1st occurrence: Verbal or written warning
- 2nd occurrence: Written or final written warning
- 3rd occurrence: Suspension or termination

Severity of consequence depends on nature and impact of violation.

### 5.4 Insubordination

**Examples:**
- Refusal to follow reasonable work directive
- Disrespectful behavior toward supervisor
- Undermining manager's authority
- Refusing assigned tasks without legitimate reason

**Typical Discipline:**
- Minor insubordination: Written warning
- Serious insubordination: Final written warning or suspension
- Gross insubordination: Immediate termination

### 5.5 Conduct and Behavior

**Examples:**
- Unprofessional behavior
- Rudeness to coworkers, customers, or vendors
- Gossip or spreading rumors
- Inappropriate language
- Creating hostile work environment

**Typical Discipline:**
- 1st occurrence: Written warning
- 2nd occurrence: Final written warning or suspension
- 3rd occurrence: Termination

Harassment, discrimination, or bullying may result in immediate termination (see Section 5.7).

### 5.6 Dishonesty and Integrity

**Examples:**
- Falsifying records or documents
- Lying to supervisor or during investigation
- Time theft (falsifying time records)
- Providing false information on application or resume
- Theft of Company or employee property
- Fraud or embezzlement
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information

**Typical Discipline:**
- Immediate termination for most violations
- Investigation and suspension pending investigation
- May involve law enforcement referral

### 5.7 Harassment, Discrimination, and Retaliation

**Examples:**
- Sexual harassment
- Harassment based on protected characteristics
- Discrimination in any form
- Retaliation against someone who reports concerns
- Creating hostile work environment
- Bullying or intimidating behavior

**Typical Discipline:**
- Immediate suspension pending investigation
- Termination if substantiated
- Mandatory training if less severe (rare)

Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, and retaliation.

### 5.8 Safety Violations

**Examples:**
- Failure to follow safety procedures
- Not wearing required personal protective equipment
- Creating unsafe conditions
- Failure to report accidents or injuries
- Operating equipment without authorization
- Horseplay or reckless behavior

**Typical Discipline:**
- Minor safety violation: Written warning
- Serious safety violation: Final written warning or suspension
- Egregious safety violation: Immediate termination

### 5.9 Substance Abuse

**Examples:**
- Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Using, possessing, or distributing illegal drugs at work
- Alcohol consumption during work hours (except approved events)
- Refusing drug/alcohol test when required

**Typical Discipline:**
- Immediate suspension pending investigation
- Termination for confirmed violation
- May be offered EAP assistance in some circumstances

### 5.10 Technology and Social Media

**Examples:**
- Accessing inappropriate websites
- Excessive personal use of company technology
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information online
- Posting disparaging comments about Company or employees
- Using Company equipment for personal business
- Installing unauthorized software

**Typical Discipline:**
- Minor violation: Written warning
- Serious violation: Final written warning or termination
- Breach of confidentiality: Immediate termination

### 5.11 Violence and Threats

**Examples:**
- Physical violence or assault
- Threats of violence
- Intimidating or threatening behavior
- Possession of weapons on Company property (except where legally authorized)
- Stalking or menacing behavior

**Typical Discipline:**
- Immediate suspension or termination
- Law enforcement involvement
- Workplace violence threat assessment
- No tolerance policy

## 6. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS

### 6.1 Investigation

Before imposing discipline (except verbal warnings):
- Gather facts about the alleged violation
- Interview relevant parties and witnesses
- Review applicable policies and documentation
- Consider employee's explanation and circumstances
- Consult with HR

**Investigation principles:**
- Prompt and thorough
- Fair and impartial
- Confidential to extent possible
- Well-documented

### 6.2 Deciding Appropriate Action

Consider the following factors:
- Severity of the violation
- Impact on Company, employees, or customers
- Employee's disciplinary history
- Length of service and overall performance
- Mitigating or aggravating circumstances
- Consistency with past practice
- Whether employee knew or should have known rule
- Intent (willful vs. negligent vs. accidental)

### 6.3 Disciplinary Meeting

**Preparation:**
- Review all facts and documentation
- Determine appropriate disciplinary action
- Prepare disciplinary documentation
- Consult with HR
- Schedule private meeting

**Meeting Structure:**
1. Explain purpose of meeting
2. Describe the violation with specific facts
3. Allow employee to respond and provide explanation
4. Explain disciplinary action and consequences
5. Outline expectations going forward
6. Review written documentation with employee
7. Answer questions
8. Have employee sign acknowledgment

**Meeting Best Practices:**
- Remain calm and professional
- Focus on facts and behavior, not personality
- Listen to employee's perspective
- Don't argue or become defensive
- Be clear and direct
- End meeting on constructive note when possible
- Have HR or witness present for serious discipline

### 6.4 Documentation Requirements

**Disciplinary documentation must include:**
- Employee name and position
- Date of violation and date of disciplinary action
- Specific description of violation (who, what, when, where)
- Which policy or rule was violated
- Employee's explanation or response
- Prior disciplinary actions (if any)
- Disciplinary action taken
- Expected corrective action
- Consequences of future violations
- Signatures (employee and manager)

**Employee Signature:**
- Signature indicates receipt, not agreement
- Employee may add written comments
- If employee refuses to sign, note refusal and have witness sign

**Document Distribution:**
- Original to personnel file
- Copy to employee
- Copy to manager's file
- Copy to HR

### 6.5 Suspension Pending Investigation

**When Used:**
- Serious allegations requiring investigation
- Safety concern or risk to others
- Potential criminal activity
- Need to preserve evidence
- Employee's presence would interfere with investigation

**Process:**
- Typically paid suspension (unless policy violation merits unpaid)
- Employee notified verbally and in writing
- Expected duration communicated
- Investigation completed promptly
- Employee notified of outcome

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Reinstatement with no discipline (if unfounded)
- Reinstatement with discipline (if substantiated)
- Termination (if violation warrants)

## 7. TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

### 7.1 Voluntary Termination

**Resignation:**
- Employee provides notice (2 weeks standard, more for senior roles)
- Submit written resignation letter
- Complete exit process
- Return Company property
- Final paycheck includes accrued PTO (if eligible per policy)

**Job Abandonment:**
- No-call, no-show for 3 consecutive days
- Considered voluntary resignation
- Employment terminated
- Documentation in file

### 7.2 Involuntary Termination

**Termination for Cause:**
- Due to misconduct or policy violation
- May follow progressive discipline or be immediate
- Final paycheck includes earned wages only (no PTO payout in most cases)
- Not eligible for rehire

**Termination for Performance:**
- Failure to meet performance standards
- Typically follows Performance Improvement Plan
- May include PTO payout per policy
- Rehire eligibility determined case-by-case

**Layoff/Reduction in Force:**
- Business-driven (not performance or conduct)
- May include severance if eligible
- Includes PTO payout per policy
- Eligible for rehire

### 7.3 Termination Process

**Manager Responsibilities:**
1. Consult with HR before termination decision
2. Review documentation supporting termination
3. Prepare termination letter
4. Schedule termination meeting (recommend day other than Friday)
5. Arrange for witness (HR representative)
6. Plan logistics (return of property, system access, final pay)

**Termination Meeting:**
- Keep meeting brief (10-15 minutes)
- State decision clearly and directly
- Provide brief reason (already documented)
- Do not argue or negotiate
- Explain final paycheck and benefits
- Collect Company property (keys, badge, laptop, phone)
- Outline next steps
- Escort from building if necessary

**What to Say:**
"We've asked you to meet with us today to inform you that your employment with Weaver Enterprises is ending, effective today. This decision is based on [brief reason - e.g., the attendance issues we discussed in your prior written warnings]. This decision is final. [HR Representative] will explain your final paycheck, benefits continuation, and return of Company property."

**What Not to Say:**
- Don't apologize (undermines decision)
- Don't go into extensive detail or debate
- Don't compare to other employees
- Don't make promises about references
- Don't provide advice on unemployment benefits

### 7.4 Final Pay and Benefits

**Final Paycheck:**
- Due on last day (or next regular payday, per state law)
- Includes all earned wages through termination date
- PTO payout per Leave Policy
- Deductions for unreturned property allowed in some states

**Benefits Continuation:**
- Health insurance ends last day of month of termination
- COBRA notice mailed within 14 days
- 401(k) account remains (employee can rollover or maintain)
- Life insurance and other benefits end per plan terms

**Return of Property:**
- Laptop, phone, tablet
- Keys, access cards, badges
- Credit cards
- Company files and documents
- Any other Company property

## 8. EMPLOYEE RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 8.1 Right to Respond

Employees have the right to:
- Provide their side of the story
- Present relevant information or evidence
- Explain circumstances or context
- Add written comments to disciplinary documentation
- Request HR involvement

### 8.2 Right to Representation

- Employees may request HR presence at disciplinary meetings
- Union employees may request union representative (if applicable)
- No right to attorney at Company disciplinary meetings

### 8.3 Appeal Process

Employees may appeal disciplinary actions:

**Who Can Appeal:**
- Any employee receiving written warning or higher
- Within 5 business days of disciplinary action

**How to Appeal:**
- Submit written appeal to HR
- State reason for appeal and supporting information
- Provide any additional evidence

**Appeal Review:**
- HR and senior leader review appeal
- May interview parties again
- Decision within 10 business days
- Appeal decision is final

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Uphold discipline
- Reduce discipline
- Remove discipline
- Request additional investigation

### 8.4 Record Retention

**Active Warnings:**
- Verbal warnings: Not in official file (manager notes only)
- Written warnings: Remain in file for 12 months
- Final written warnings: Remain in file for 24 months
- Suspensions: Remain in file permanently
- Terminations: Remain in file permanently

**Removal of Warnings:**
- After expiration period, may be removed from "active" file
- Moved to separate file but retained per retention policy
- Still considered if future violations occur during active period

## 9. MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

### 9.1 Addressing Issues Promptly

Managers should:
- Address issues as they arise
- Not ignore or delay discipline
- Provide real-time feedback
- Document performance and conduct concerns
- Escalate to HR when appropriate

### 9.2 Consistency and Fairness

Managers must:
- Apply policies consistently
- Treat similar situations similarly
- Avoid favoritism or bias
- Document all disciplinary actions
- Consult HR for guidance

### 9.3 Required HR Involvement

Managers must consult HR before:
- Issuing final written warning
- Suspending employee
- Terminating employee
- Addressing potential harassment or discrimination
- Situations involving legal risk

HR approval required for:
- All terminations
- All suspensions
- Final written warnings

## 10. HR RESPONSIBILITIES

HR is responsible for:
- Providing guidance on disciplinary process
- Ensuring legal compliance
- Reviewing disciplinary documentation
- Participating in serious disciplinary meetings
- Conducting or overseeing investigations
- Maintaining disciplinary records
- Training managers on disciplinary procedures
- Monitoring for consistency across organization
- Handling appeals
