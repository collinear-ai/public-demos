## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes the framework for onboarding new employees at Weaver Enterprises. A comprehensive onboarding program helps new hires integrate successfully, become productive quickly, and feel welcomed into our company culture.

This policy applies to all new employees, including full-time, part-time, temporary, and contract workers. Special provisions for remote employees are detailed in Section 8.

## 2. ONBOARDING PHILOSOPHY

Weaver Enterprises believes that effective onboarding:
- Begins before the first day and continues through the first year
- Is a shared responsibility among HR, managers, and team members
- Sets clear expectations while providing necessary support
- Creates connections and builds relationships
- Accelerates time-to-productivity while reducing early turnover

Our goal is for every new employee to feel welcomed, prepared, and confident in their new role.

## 3. PRE-BOARDING (BEFORE FIRST DAY)

### 3.1 HR Responsibilities

**Upon Acceptance of Offer:**
- Send official offer letter and employment agreement
- Initiate background check and verify completion
- Send new hire paperwork packet (within 3 business days of acceptance)
- Create employee record in HRIS system
- Assign employee ID number
- Notify IT of new hire for equipment provisioning
- Notify Facilities for workspace setup (if applicable)

**New Hire Paperwork Packet Includes:**
- Welcome letter from CEO
- Employee handbook acknowledgment
- I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification form
- W-4 Federal Tax Withholding form
- State tax withholding form (where applicable)
- Direct deposit authorization form
- Emergency contact form
- Benefits enrollment guide
- Confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement
- Intellectual property agreement
- Company policy acknowledgments
- Parking/badge request forms (if applicable)

**One Week Before Start Date:**
- Send "First Day Information" email including:
  - Start time and location (or virtual meeting link for remote)
  - Parking instructions
  - What to bring (ID for I-9, completed paperwork)
  - Dress code
  - First day schedule overview
  - Manager and HR contact information
- Confirm all equipment and workspace ready
- Send calendar invites for Day 1 activities

### 3.2 Manager Responsibilities

**Upon Hire Decision:**
- Partner with HR on offer details
- Identify onboarding buddy/mentor for new hire
- Plan first week schedule and assignments
- Schedule meet-and-greets with key stakeholders

**One Week Before Start Date:**
- Send personal welcome message to new hire
- Prepare workspace (or verify remote setup)
- Create 30-60-90 day plan outline
- Brief team on new hire's start date and role
- Schedule regular one-on-ones for first 90 days

### 3.3 IT Responsibilities

**Equipment Provisioning:**
- Order laptop/computer based on role requirements
- Configure email and system access
- Set up required software and applications
- Create accounts for all necessary systems
- Prepare security badge (if applicable)
- Ship equipment to remote employees (arrive 2-3 days before start)

## 4. WELCOME PACKAGE

### 4.1 Welcome Package Contents

All new employees receive a welcome package containing:

**Company Materials:**
- Welcome card signed by team members
- Company branded merchandise:
  - T-shirt or polo
  - Water bottle
  - Notebook and pen
  - Laptop bag or backpack
  - Stickers/decals
- Employee handbook (printed or digital)
- Organization chart (see `org_chart.md`)
- Office map (for on-site employees)
- Company directory/who's who guide

**IT Equipment:**
- Laptop computer (configured and ready)
- Monitor (for applicable roles)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset (for roles requiring calls)
- Laptop stand (upon request)
- Any role-specific equipment

**Access Cards/Credentials:**
- Building access badge
- Parking pass (if applicable)
- Temporary password cards for initial login

### 4.2 Welcome Package Delivery

**On-Site Employees:**
- Package placed on desk before arrival
- IT equipment set up and ready to use
- Badge and parking pass available at reception

**Remote Employees:**
- Package shipped to home address
- Target delivery: 2-3 business days before start date
- Tracking information provided
- IT equipment pre-configured with remote access
- Setup instructions included

### 4.3 Welcome Email

New hires receive a welcome email from HR on their first day containing:
- Link to employee self-service portal
- Instructions for initial system login
- Benefits enrollment instructions and deadlines
- List of required training courses
- Key contacts (HR, IT Help Desk, Manager)
- First week schedule
- Link to company intranet and resources

## 6. FIRST WEEK ONBOARDING

### 6.1 First Week Goals

By end of Week 1, new employees should:
- Complete all required paperwork
- Have full system access
- Understand their role and initial priorities
- Meet key team members and stakeholders
- Complete Day 1 required training
- Know where to find resources and help
- Feel welcomed and connected

### 6.2 First Week Activities

**Day 2:**
- Complete mandatory compliance training
- Deeper dive into role with manager
- Begin reviewing documentation and materials
- Continue meeting team members

**Day 3:**
- Start initial assignments or shadowing
- Lunch with onboarding buddy
- Continue required training modules
- Department/team overview meeting

**Day 4:**
- Continue hands-on work or training
- Meet with key stakeholders
- Review team processes and tools
- Check-in with manager

**Day 5:**
- Week 1 reflection meeting with manager
- Complete first week survey
- Finalize benefits enrollment (if not done)
- Set priorities for Week 2

### 6.3 Required Training (Week 1)

All new employees must complete within first week:

| Training | Duration | Delivery |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Company Overview and Culture | 1 hour | Video/eLearning |
| Code of Conduct | 30 min | eLearning |
| Anti-Harassment and Discrimination | 1 hour | eLearning |
| Information Security Awareness | 45 min | eLearning |
| Data Privacy (GDPR/CCPA) | 30 min | eLearning |
| Workplace Safety | 30 min | eLearning |
| Benefits Enrollment Overview | 30 min | Video |

Additional role-specific training assigned by manager.

## 7. 30-60-90 DAY ONBOARDING PLAN

### 7.1 First 30 Days: Learn

**Focus Areas:**
- Understanding the role and responsibilities
- Learning company culture and values
- Building relationships with team and stakeholders
- Completing all required training
- Getting up to speed on tools and processes

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Weekly one-on-ones (minimum)
- Clear direction on initial projects
- Regular feedback and guidance
- 30-day check-in meeting
- Address questions and concerns promptly

**New Hire Expectations:**
- Complete all required training
- Meet with key stakeholders
- Begin contributing to team projects
- Ask questions and seek help when needed
- Document learnings and questions

**30-Day Checkpoint:**
- Formal meeting with manager
- Review progress against expectations
- Discuss any challenges or concerns
- Adjust plan if needed
- Complete 30-day feedback survey

### 7.2 Days 31-60: Contribute

**Focus Areas:**
- Taking ownership of assigned projects
- Applying learning to real work
- Deepening relationships and collaboration
- Identifying areas for growth
- Providing value to the team

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Continue regular one-on-ones
- Provide stretch assignments
- Give ongoing feedback
- Connect new hire to broader organization
- 60-day check-in meeting

**New Hire Expectations:**
- Own and complete assigned projects
- Proactively identify improvements
- Collaborate effectively with team
- Seek feedback and act on it
- Continue building network

**60-Day Checkpoint:**
- Review accomplishments and progress
- Discuss growth and development
- Identify any performance concerns early
- Set goals for final 30 days
- Complete 60-day feedback survey

### 7.3 Days 61-90: Own

**Focus Areas:**
- Fully performing in role
- Operating independently
- Contributing meaningfully to team goals
- Building expertise and credibility
- Identifying long-term development goals

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Transition to standard one-on-one cadence
- Provide constructive performance feedback
- Discuss career development
- 90-day performance review
- Confirm successful completion of probation

**New Hire Expectations:**
- Perform role independently
- Meet performance expectations
- Demonstrate company values
- Develop expertise in area
- Contribute ideas and improvements

**90-Day Review:**
- Formal performance assessment
- Document strengths and development areas
- Confirm successful completion of probationary period
- Set goals for remainder of year
- Complete 90-day onboarding survey
- Transition to regular performance management

### 7.4 Probationary Period

**Duration:** 90 days from start date

**Purpose:**
- Assess fit and performance
- Provide intensive support and feedback
- Allow employee to evaluate fit

**Expectations:**
- Employee demonstrates ability to perform role
- Employee exhibits company values
- No significant performance or conduct concerns
- Mutual agreement to continue employment

**Outcomes:**
- **Successful:** Employment continues; transition to regular status
- **Extended:** Additional 30-60 days if more time needed
- **Unsuccessful:** Employment may be terminated

## 8. REMOTE EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING

### 8.1 Remote Onboarding Principles

Remote onboarding requires extra intentionality to:
- Create connection despite physical distance
- Ensure access to all necessary tools and information
- Provide adequate support and communication
- Build relationships with team members

### 8.2 Equipment and Setup

**Pre-Start Equipment Shipment:**
- All equipment shipped to arrive 2-3 days before start
- Shipping tracked and confirmed
- Include setup instructions and IT support contact
- Test equipment works before first day

**Home Office Requirements:**
- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps)
- Quiet, dedicated workspace
- Appropriate desk and chair
- Good lighting for video calls

**Home Office Stipend:**
- One-time $500 stipend for home office setup (new remote hires)
- Monthly $75 internet/phone stipend
- Submit receipts through expense system
- See Remote Work Policy for details

### 8.3 Virtual Orientation

**Day 1 Virtual Welcome:**
- Video call with HR for orientation
- Virtual building/team tour video
- Video introductions with team members
- Virtual coffee chat with onboarding buddy

**Virtual Onboarding Best Practices:**
- Camera on for all meetings (when possible)
- Use collaboration tools (Slack, Teams) actively
- Schedule informal virtual coffee chats
- Over-communicate in first weeks
- Record important meetings for review

### 8.4 Remote-Specific First Week

**Additional Activities:**
- IT walk-through of remote access tools
- VPN and security setup verification
- Communication tools training (Slack, Zoom)
- Virtual meet-and-greets scheduled throughout week
- Daily end-of-day check-in with manager

**Manager Best Practices for Remote Onboarding:**
- Schedule more frequent check-ins (daily first week)
- Be available and responsive
- Use video calls when possible
- Create opportunities for informal connection
- Pair with experienced remote team member as buddy

### 8.5 Building Remote Connections

**Onboarding Buddy Program:**
- Assigned peer mentor for first 90 days
- Weekly informal check-ins
- Resource for questions and guidance
- Helps new hire navigate culture and systems

**Virtual Team Building:**
- Invite to all team social events (virtual happy hours, games)
- Schedule virtual lunch with team members
- Add to relevant Slack channels
- Include in team traditions and rituals

## 9. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 9.1 HR Responsibilities

- Design and maintain onboarding program
- Coordinate pre-boarding activities
- Conduct HR orientation
- Process all new hire paperwork
- Track completion of required activities
- Survey new hires and gather feedback
- Continuously improve onboarding program

### 9.2 Manager Responsibilities

- Prepare for new hire's arrival
- Create 30-60-90 day plan
- Conduct regular check-ins
- Provide clear expectations and feedback
- Connect new hire with team and stakeholders
- Conduct 30, 60, and 90-day reviews
- Support new hire's success

### 9.3 IT Responsibilities

- Provision equipment and access
- Configure systems and software
- Provide IT orientation and support
- Troubleshoot technical issues
- Ensure security protocols followed

### 9.4 Onboarding Buddy Responsibilities

- Serve as peer resource and guide
- Answer questions about culture and norms
- Help new hire navigate the organization
- Provide informal support and connection
- Meet regularly (at least weekly) for first 90 days

### 9.5 New Hire Responsibilities

- Complete all required paperwork timely
- Actively engage in onboarding activities
- Ask questions and seek help when needed
- Complete required training
- Build relationships proactively
- Provide honest feedback on onboarding experience
