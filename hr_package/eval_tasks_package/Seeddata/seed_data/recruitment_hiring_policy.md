## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes guidelines and procedures for recruiting, selecting, and hiring qualified employees at Weaver Enterprises Inc. The purpose is to:

- Attract and hire highly qualified candidates
- Ensure fair, consistent, and legally compliant hiring practices
- Promote diversity and equal employment opportunity
- Create positive candidate experiences
- Support business objectives through strategic talent acquisition

This policy applies to all hiring activities for regular full-time, part-time, temporary, and intern positions across all departments and locations.

## 2. EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY

### 2.1 Non-Discrimination Commitment

Weaver Enterprises is an Equal Opportunity Employer. All employment decisions are made without regard to:
- Race, color, ethnicity, national origin
- Sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Religion, creed
- Age
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Genetic information
- Pregnancy or related conditions
- Military or veteran status
- Marital status
- Any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law

### 2.2 Affirmative Action

The Company is committed to affirmative action and takes proactive steps to ensure equal opportunity in recruitment, hiring, and advancement. Specific affirmative action plans are maintained as required by federal contractors regulations.

### 2.3 Reasonable Accommodation

The Company provides reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities during the application and interview process. Candidates requiring accommodations should contact the HR Recruiting team.

## 3. HIRING AUTHORITY AND APPROVAL

### 3.1 Position Authorization

Before recruiting for any position:
- Department head must obtain budget approval
- Submit headcount request to HR with business justification
- HR reviews and obtains necessary approvals (Finance, Executive Leadership)
- Position approved for posting once all approvals obtained

### 3.2 Job Requisition Process

**Hiring Manager Responsibilities:**
1. Complete job requisition form in applicant tracking system
2. Provide or update job description
3. Specify required qualifications and preferences
4. Indicate salary range and position level
5. Submit to HR for review

**HR Review:**
- Verify budget availability
- Review job description for compliance and clarity
- Confirm compensation is market-competitive
- Approve requisition or return for revisions
- Post approved positions internally and externally

## 4. JOB DESCRIPTIONS

### 4.1 Purpose of Job Descriptions

Job descriptions serve as foundation for:
- Recruiting and screening candidates
- Setting performance expectations
- Determining compensation levels
- Conducting performance evaluations
- Compliance with employment laws (ADA, FLSA)

### 4.2 Required Components

All job descriptions must include:
- Job title and department
- FLSA classification (exempt or non-exempt)
- Reporting relationship
- Position summary
- Essential job functions and duties
- Required qualifications (education, experience, skills)
- Preferred qualifications
- Physical requirements and working conditions
- Travel requirements (if applicable)

### 4.3 Creating and Updating Job Descriptions

- HR maintains template and assists with development
- Hiring managers draft or update descriptions
- HR reviews for compliance and consistency
- Approved job descriptions stored in HR system
- Review and update annually or when position changes significantly

## 5. SOURCING AND RECRUITING

### 5.1 Internal Posting

**Internal Candidates First:**
- All positions posted internally for minimum 5 business days before external posting
- Posted on internal careers portal and distributed via employee email
- Current employees encouraged to apply for advancement opportunities

**Internal Applicant Process:**
- Employees apply through internal portal
- Notify current manager before applying (encouraged but not required)
- If selected, coordinate transition timeline with both managers
- HR facilitates internal transfers

**Internal Transfer Guidelines:**
- Employees must be in current role for 12 months before applying for new position
- Exception: restructuring, elimination, or manager approval
- Performance must be satisfactory (not on performance improvement plan)

### 5.2 External Recruiting Sources

**Company Career Website:**
- Primary external recruiting source
- Integrated with applicant tracking system
- Mobile-optimized application process

**Job Boards:**
- General: Indeed, LinkedIn, Glassdoor
- Industry-specific: As appropriate for role
- Diversity-focused: Ability Jobs, Veterans Job Board, diversity professional associations

**Employee Referrals:**
- Employees encouraged to refer qualified candidates
- Referral bonus program (see Section 5.3)
- Referrals receive priority review

**Recruiting Agencies:**
- Used for specialized or hard-to-fill roles
- Must be pre-approved vendor
- Contingency or retained search depending on level
- Typical fees: 20-25% of first-year compensation

**Campus Recruiting:**
- Target universities for entry-level and intern hiring
- Career fairs, on-campus interviews, info sessions
- Internship-to-full-time pipeline

**Social Media:**
- LinkedIn recruiting and company page
- Twitter, Facebook for employer branding
- Showcase company culture and opportunities

**Professional Associations:**
- Partner with industry organizations
- Sponsor events and job boards
- Attend conferences for networking

### 5.3 Employee Referral Program

**Referral Bonus Structure:**
- Entry-level positions: $500
- Mid-level positions: $1,000
- Senior/specialized positions: $2,000
- Executive positions: $3,000

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Referring employee must be in good standing
- Referred candidate hired as regular employee
- Referred candidate completes 90-day probationary period
- Bonus paid after successful completion of probation

**Process:**
- Submit referral through employee portal
- Include candidate name, contact info, and position of interest
- HR screens referred candidate
- Referrer notified of hiring decision

## 6. APPLICATION PROCESS

### 6.1 Online Application System

All external candidates must apply through the company's applicant tracking system:
- Submit resume/CV
- Complete application form
- Answer pre-screening questions
- Provide voluntary EEO demographic information (kept confidential, not used in hiring decisions)
- Submit cover letter (optional but encouraged)

### 6.2 Application Materials

**Required:**
- Resume or CV
- Completed application form

**Optional:**
- Cover letter
- Portfolio or work samples (for applicable positions)
- References (typically requested later in process)

### 6.3 Application Deadline

- Applications accepted until position filled
- Posting may close early if sufficient qualified candidates
- Candidates notified if position filled

### 6.4 Application Confirmation

- Automated confirmation email sent upon submission
- Includes timeline expectations
- Provides contact information for questions

## 7. CANDIDATE SCREENING AND SELECTION

### 7.1 Resume Review

**HR Initial Screening:**
- Review all applications for minimum qualifications
- Disqualify clearly unqualified candidates
- Forward qualified candidates to hiring manager
- Timeline: Within 3-5 business days of application

**Hiring Manager Review:**
- Evaluate candidates against position requirements
- Select candidates for phone screening
- Provide feedback to HR on selected candidates

### 7.2 Phone Screening

**Purpose:**
- Assess basic qualifications and fit
- Clarify experience and career goals
- Explain position and company
- Gauge candidate interest and availability
- Determine if in-person interview warranted

**Conducted By:** HR Recruiter or Hiring Manager

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes

**Key Topics:**
- Work history and relevant experience
- Interest in position and company
- Salary expectations
- Availability and timeline
- Work authorization status

### 7.3 Interview Process

**Interview Stages:**

**First-Round Interview:**
- Hiring manager and 1-2 team members
- In-person, video, or phone (typically video for remote roles)
- Duration: 45-60 minutes
- Focus: Skills, experience, cultural fit

**Second-Round Interview:**
- Additional team members and stakeholders
- May include senior leadership
- Duration: 2-4 hours (multiple interviews)
- May include skills assessment, presentation, or case study

**Final Interview:**
- Department head or executive leadership
- Reserved for finalist candidates
- Focus: Leadership potential, strategic thinking, culture alignment
- Duration: 30-60 minutes

**Panel Interviews:**
- Used when appropriate
- 2-4 interviewers
- Coordinated questions to avoid redundancy
- Consolidated feedback

### 7.4 Interview Guidelines

**Professional Conduct:**
- Treat all candidates with respect and professionalism
- Start and end on time
- Provide clear information about next steps

**Structured Interviews:**
- Use standardized questions for all candidates
- Focus on job-related competencies
- Behavioral questions preferred ("Tell me about a time when...")
- Avoid hypothetical questions when possible

**Legal Compliance:**
- Ask only job-related questions
- Avoid questions about protected characteristics
- Do not ask about arrest records (conviction records permissible)
- Do not require disclosure of disabilities (may discuss accommodations needed to perform essential functions)

**Interview Panel Diversity:**
- Strive for diverse interview panels
- Multiple perspectives improve hiring decisions
- Reduces potential for bias

## 8. CANDIDATE ASSESSMENTS

### 8.1 Skills Testing

Pre-employment testing may be used when job-related:
- Technical skills assessments (coding, software proficiency)
- Writing samples
- Presentations or case studies
- Personality and work style assessments (advisory only, not determinative)

**Testing Requirements:**
- Must be validated and job-related
- Applied consistently to all candidates for position
- Accommodations provided for disabilities
- Results kept confidential

### 8.2 Work Samples and Assignments

- May request work samples or complete assignments
- Must be reasonable in scope (typically 1-3 hours maximum)
- Provide clear instructions and expectations
- Compensate if assignment is substantial or results used by Company

### 8.3 Cognitive and Personality Assessments

- Used as one data point in hiring decision
- Cannot be sole basis for decision
- Must be validated for position
- Results shared with candidate upon request

## 9. BACKGROUND CHECKS AND SCREENING

### 9.1 Types of Background Checks

**Standard Background Check (All Positions):**
- Criminal history (7 years)
- Employment verification
- Education verification
- Professional license verification (if applicable)

**Extended Background Check (Leadership and Sensitive Positions):**
- Credit history (for financial roles only)
- Driving record (for driving positions)
- Global sanctions and watch lists

### 9.2 Background Check Process

**Timing:**
- Conducted after conditional offer extended
- Must be completed before start date
- Offer contingent on satisfactory results

**Procedure:**
1. Candidate receives conditional offer
2. HR sends background check authorization and disclosure
3. Candidate completes authorization form
4. Third-party vendor conducts check
5. Results reviewed by HR
6. Adverse action process followed if necessary (see 9.3)

**Candidate Rights:**
- Receive pre-adverse action notice if concerns arise
- Opportunity to dispute or explain findings
- Receive copy of background report
- Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) rights provided

### 9.3 Adverse Action Process

If background check reveals disqualifying information:

1. **Pre-Adverse Action:**
   - Provide candidate with copy of report
   - Give notice of intent to take adverse action
   - Allow 5 business days to respond
   - Review any explanation or disputes

2. **Final Adverse Action:**
   - If decision to rescind offer, send adverse action notice
   - Include copy of report and FCRA rights
   - Document decision rationale

### 9.4 Criminal History Considerations

**Ban the Box Compliance:**
- Do not ask about criminal history on application
- Discuss only after conditional offer
- Consider nature, recency, and relevance of conviction
- Provide opportunity for individualized assessment

**Disqualifying Convictions:**
- Convictions directly related to job responsibilities
- Recent violent crimes (within 7 years)
- Determined on case-by-case basis
- Legal consultation for complex situations

### 9.5 Drug Testing

Drug testing conducted post-offer for:
- Safety-sensitive positions (as defined by DOT or company policy)
- Positions requiring operation of machinery or vehicles
- Positions with security clearance requirements

**Testing Protocol:**
- Urine drug screen (5- or 10-panel)
- Conducted at certified lab
- Medical Review Officer (MRO) reviews results
- Positive results may disqualify candidate

## 10. REFERENCE CHECKS

### 10.1 When to Conduct Reference Checks

- After final interview for top candidates
- Before extending offer
- Minimum 2-3 professional references

### 10.2 Reference Check Process

**Conducted By:** HR or Hiring Manager

**Information to Gather:**
- Relationship to candidate and dates
- Job responsibilities and performance
- Strengths and areas for development
- Attendance and reliability
- Eligibility for rehire
- Any concerns or additional comments

**Documentation:**
- Complete reference check form for each reference
- Document all responses
- Maintain in recruitment file

### 10.3 Providing References for Former Employees

When contacted for reference on former Weaver Enterprises employees:
- Confirm dates of employment and title only
- Direct all reference requests to HR
- Do not provide performance information without signed authorization
- HR may provide additional information with proper authorization

## 11. OFFER PROCESS

### 11.1 Salary Determination

**Factors Considered:**
- Market data and salary ranges for position
- Candidate qualifications and experience
- Internal equity (compared to current employees)
- Budget constraints
- Candidate's salary expectations

**Approval Requirements:**
- Offers within approved range: Hiring manager approval
- Offers above range: HR and Finance approval required
- Executive positions: CEO approval required

### 11.2 Offer Letter Components

Written offer letters include:
- Job title and department
- Start date
- Compensation (salary or hourly rate)
- Bonus or commission structure (if applicable)
- Benefits summary
- Work location and schedule
- Reporting relationship
- Employment-at-will statement
- Contingencies (background check, drug test, I-9 verification)
- Offer expiration date (typically 3-5 business days)

### 11.3 Extending the Offer

**Process:**
1. HR prepares offer letter
2. Obtain necessary approvals
3. Hiring manager makes verbal offer by phone
4. HR sends written offer via email
5. Candidate reviews and accepts or declines
6. Candidate returns signed offer letter

**Verbal Offer:**
- Discuss all terms clearly
- Answer questions
- Confirm candidate acceptance before sending written offer
- Reiterate offer is contingent on background check

**Negotiation:**
- Some negotiation expected for senior roles
- Stay within approved salary range when possible
- Consult HR before revising offer
- Document final agreed-upon terms

### 11.4 Offer Acceptance

- Candidate signs and returns offer letter
- HR processes new hire paperwork
- Hiring manager notified of acceptance
- Begin onboarding process

### 11.5 Offer Decline

If candidate declines:
- Request feedback on reason
- Document in ATS
- Return to candidate pool or restart search
- Notify interview team

## 12. PRE-EMPLOYMENT REQUIREMENTS

### 12.1 Form I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification

**Timing:** Within 3 business days of start date

**Process:**
- Employee completes Section 1 on or before first day
- Employee presents acceptable documents (List A, or List B + List C)
- HR completes Section 2 within 3 business days
- Documents examined in person (or via authorized remote verification)

**Acceptable Documents:**
- List A: Passport, Permanent Resident Card, Employment Authorization Document
- List B: Driver's license, State ID, School ID with photo
- List C: Social Security card, Birth certificate

**E-Verify:**
- Company participates in E-Verify program
- Cases created within 3 business days of hire
- Resolves tentative non-confirmations with employee assistance

### 12.2 Tax Forms

**Required Forms:**
- Form W-4 (Federal withholding)
- State withholding forms (as applicable)
- Direct deposit authorization

**Timing:** Completed on or before first day of employment

### 12.3 Pre-Employment Paperwork

**New Hire Packet Includes:**
- Emergency contact information
- Benefits enrollment forms
- Employee handbook acknowledgment
- Code of conduct acknowledgment
- Confidentiality/IP agreements (for applicable positions)
- Arbitration agreement (where permitted by law)
- Company policies acknowledgments

## 13. CANDIDATE COMMUNICATION

### 13.1 Timely Communication

- Acknowledge applications within 24 hours (automated)
- Update candidates within 2 weeks of each interview stage
- Provide clear timeline expectations
- Be responsive to candidate questions

### 13.2 Rejection Communication

**When to Notify:**
- After resume screening
- After phone screen
- After in-person interview

**How to Notify:**
- Email for early-stage rejections
- Phone call for final-round candidates (encouraged)
- Use professional, respectful language
- Offer general feedback when appropriate (avoid detailed critique)

**Feedback Guidelines:**
- Keep feedback high-level and constructive
- Focus on qualifications and fit, not personal characteristics
- Avoid detailed explanations that could be legally problematic
- Encourage candidate to apply for future opportunities

### 13.3 Candidate Experience

**Best Practices:**
- Respond promptly to inquiries
- Provide clear job descriptions and expectations
- Maintain professional, respectful communication
- Coordinate interview schedules efficiently
- Reimburse travel expenses for final-round candidates (when applicable)
- Send thank-you notes after interviews
- Provide feedback when requested (within appropriate limits)

## 14. RECORDKEEPING AND COMPLIANCE

### 14.1 Applicant Tracking

- All recruitment activity documented in the applicant tracking system (ATS)
- Includes: applications, resumes, interview notes, assessments, correspondence
- Ensures compliance with recordkeeping requirements
- Supports data-driven recruiting decisions

### 14.2 Required Records

**Maintain for All Applicants:**
- Application materials
- Resumes/CVs
- Interview evaluations
- Assessment results
- Reference check notes
- Hiring decision rationale

**Retention Period:**
- Hired employees: Duration of employment + 7 years
- Not hired: 3 years from application date (or 2 years for federal contractors)
- Adverse impact analyses: 2 years

### 14.3 EEO and Affirmative Action Reporting

**EEO-1 Report:**
- Annual report to EEOC
- Employee demographics by job category
- Due: September 30 each year

**AAP Analysis:**
- Annual utilization analysis
- Applicant flow data
- Adverse impact analysis
- Goal setting and action plans

**OFCCP Compliance:**
- Internet applicant records
- Hiring statistics by protected class
- Self-identification data (voluntary)

### 14.4 Audits and Investigations

**Internal Audits:**
- HR conducts periodic recruiting compliance audits
- Review job descriptions, postings, interview questions
- Analyze hiring data for potential adverse impact
- Corrective action taken as needed

**External Audits:**
- Cooperate with OFCCP, EEOC, or other agency reviews
- Provide requested documentation
- Legal counsel involved in responses

## 15. SPECIAL HIRING SITUATIONS

### 15.1 Rehiring Former Employees

**Eligibility:**
- Former employees eligible for rehire if left in good standing
- Not eligible if terminated for cause
- HR reviews personnel file before rehiring

**Process:**
- Follow standard application and interview process
- May expedite screening if recently separated (within 6 months)
- New I-9 required if more than 3 years since termination
- No break in service credit for benefits (treated as new hire)

### 15.2 Nepotism and Personal Relationships

**Policy:**
- Company permits hiring of relatives but with restrictions
- Relatives cannot be in direct reporting relationship
- Cannot work in same department if one supervises the other

**Disclosure Required:**
- Candidates must disclose existing relationships with employees
- HR evaluates potential conflicts

**Definitions:**
- Relatives: spouse, domestic partner, parent, child, sibling, in-laws, step-relatives

### 15.3 Contingent Workers

**Temporary Employees:**
- Hired through approved staffing agencies
- Contract duration specified upfront
- May be converted to regular employee after 6 months

**Independent Contractors:**
- Must meet IRS independent contractor criteria
- Separate contracting process (not covered by this policy)
- Legal review required

### 15.4 Interns

**Internship Program:**
- Paid internships (comply with FLSA)
- College students or recent graduates
- 10-12 week summer program or part-time during school year
- Conversion to full-time considered based on performance

**Process:**
- Campus recruiting and online applications
- Structured interview process
- Educational experience with meaningful projects
- Mentor assigned

## 16. HIRING MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

Hiring managers are responsible for:
- Initiating requisitions and obtaining approvals
- Reviewing resumes and selecting candidates
- Conducting interviews in professional, compliant manner
- Providing timely feedback to HR
- Participating in hiring decisions
- Coordinating with HR on offers and negotiations
- Preparing for new hire onboarding
- Conducting new hire orientation for team
