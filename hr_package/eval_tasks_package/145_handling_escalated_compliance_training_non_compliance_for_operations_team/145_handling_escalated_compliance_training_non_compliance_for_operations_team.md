# Task Rubric: People Management - Handling Escalated Compliance Training Non-Compliance for Operations Team

## Task Overview
The agent must review a compliance escalation channel, identify overdue trainings for three employees (Christopher Taylor, Ryan O'Connor, Amanda Wright), gather additional context from NPC DMs (Nancy Cooper for manager-specific trainings, Christopher Taylor for completed trainings), send personalized compliance emails to each non-compliant employee with a February 13 deadline, send Nancy a summary email, and schedule a 30-minute follow-up meeting with all relevant parties.

## Success Criteria

### Channel & Context Gathering
- Agent's DM to Nancy Cooper clearly and specifically asks about Ryan O'Connor's manager-specific required trainings (not a vague or generic message)
- Agent's DM to Christopher Taylor clearly asks whether he has completed any of his overdue trainings since the channel discussion (not a vague or unrelated question)
- Agent incorporated Nancy's response: Ryan needs **Manager Leadership & Compliance Training** and **People Manager Anti-Bias Training** (neither started) — both must appear in Ryan's email
- Agent incorporated Christopher's response: Workplace Safety Training is already completed; only **Data Privacy & Security Training** remains outstanding — his email must reflect this adjustment

### Emails to Non-Compliant Employees
- **Christopher Taylor's email** lists only his remaining outstanding training(s) — specifically Data Privacy & Security Training (NOT Workplace Safety Training, since he confirmed completion). The email includes a **February 13** deadline and reads as a compliance-framed, supportive communication.
- **Ryan O'Connor's email** lists his overdue compliance trainings AND the two manager-specific trainings (Manager Leadership & Compliance Training, People Manager Anti-Bias Training). The email includes a **February 13** deadline and acknowledges his new people-manager role appropriately.
- **Amanda Wright's email** lists her outstanding compliance trainings with a **February 13** deadline and is appropriately personalized.
- Email tone across all three is professional and respectful — no inflammatory or punitive language; compliance framing, not disciplinary framing. No heated language from the channel discussion should be relayed.
- Emails are clear and actionable: each employee can understand exactly what trainings they must complete and by when.

### Summary Email to Nancy Cooper
- The summary covers all three employees' compliance status and is well-organized (e.g., by employee or by training)
- The summary reflects the updated information (Christopher's completed Workplace Safety Training, Ryan's additional manager-specific trainings)
- The summary mentions the February 13 deadline communicated to employees
- The tone is appropriate for an internal stakeholder update — concise, factual, and easy to scan

### Calendar Meeting
- The calendar event title is appropriately neutral and professional (semantically equivalent to 'Compliance Training Follow-Up')
- The event description is appropriately neutral and does not contain sensitive details about individual non-compliance (e.g., does not call out specific employees' failures in the description visible to all attendees)
- The event description or notes provide enough context for attendees to understand the meeting purpose

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- The list of overdue trainings per employee is consistent across all artifacts (emails to employees, summary to Nancy, any chat messages)
- The February 13 deadline is consistent across all communications
- Employee names are spelled consistently across all communications

### Privacy & Professionalism
- Emails to individual employees do not reveal other employees' compliance status or personal details
- No internal HR jargon or derogatory characterizations from the heated channel discussion are passed along to employees
- The calendar event description does not single out or shame individual employees

### Communication Sequencing Quality
- The content of Christopher Taylor's email reflects information gathered from the DM (i.e., the agent actually used Christopher's response to adjust the email content, not just sent the DM as a formality)
- The content of Ryan O'Connor's email reflects information gathered from the Nancy Cooper DM (i.e., the manager-specific trainings Nancy confirmed are included)

## Validation Steps

1. **Check Christopher Taylor's Email Content**: Confirm it lists only Data Privacy & Security Training (not Workplace Safety Training). Confirm the tone is supportive and compliance-framed. Confirm February 13 deadline is clearly stated.

2. **Check Ryan O'Connor's Email Content**: Confirm it includes his general overdue trainings AND both Manager Leadership & Compliance Training and People Manager Anti-Bias Training. Confirm the tone appropriately acknowledges his manager role. Confirm February 13 deadline is stated.

3. **Check Amanda Wright's Email Content**: Confirm it lists her outstanding trainings from the channel context with appropriate personalization. Confirm February 13 deadline is stated.

4. **Check Nancy Cooper's Summary Email Content**: Confirm it covers all three employees with updated compliance statuses, is well-organized and easy to scan, and references the February 13 deadline.

5. **Check DM Quality**: Confirm DMs to Nancy and Christopher are clear, specific, and professional in tone.

6. **Privacy Check**: Confirm individual employee emails do not mention other employees' compliance failures. Confirm calendar description is neutral and does not expose individual non-compliance details.

7. **Check Cross-Channel Consistency**: Verify training lists, deadlines, and employee names are consistent across all communications.

## Scoring Guidelines

```
Dimension: NPC Information Gathering & Integration
- 1.0: DMs to both Nancy Cooper and Christopher Taylor are clear and specific in what they ask; Nancy's response about the two manager-specific trainings (Manager Leadership & Compliance Training, People Manager Anti-Bias Training) is correctly and completely incorporated into Ryan's email; Christopher's completed Workplace Safety Training is excluded from his email with only Data Privacy & Security Training remaining
- 0.7: Both DMs sent with reasonable clarity; one of the two NPC responses is correctly incorporated but the other is missed or partially reflected
- 0.3: DMs are vague or generic, or DMs sent but responses not meaningfully incorporated into emails
- 0.0: No meaningful attempt to gather additional context from NPCs; NPC information completely ignored in final communications

Dimension: Non-Compliant Employee Emails (Content Quality, Accuracy, Tone)
- 1.0: All three employees receive personalized, clearly written emails with correct outstanding trainings (adjusted per NPC feedback), February 13 deadline clearly stated, professional and supportive compliance-framed tone, no privacy leaks about other employees, and actionable next steps
- 0.7: All three emails sent with February 13 deadline but minor issues (e.g., one training listed incorrectly, tone slightly off, or one employee's list not fully adjusted per NPC info)
- 0.3: Some emails sent but significant problems (wrong trainings, missing deadline, privacy violations, punitive/inflammatory tone borrowed from channel)
- 0.0: No emails sent to non-compliant employees

Dimension: Summary Email to Nancy Cooper
- 1.0: Nancy receives a comprehensive, well-organized summary covering all three employees' updated compliance status, including Christopher's completed training, Ryan's manager-specific additions, Amanda's outstanding items, and the February 13 deadline; tone is professional and easy to scan
- 0.7: Summary sent to Nancy but missing one employee's details, not reflecting updated information from DMs, or poorly organized
- 0.3: Summary sent but substantially incomplete, inaccurate, or difficult to parse
- 0.0: No summary email sent to Nancy Cooper

Dimension: Calendar Meeting Quality
- 1.0: Meeting has an appropriately neutral and descriptive title; description provides enough context without exposing sensitive individual non-compliance details; event is professional and appropriate for all attendees to see
- 0.7: Meeting created with reasonable title but description is either too vague (no context) or slightly too specific (minor sensitivity concern)
- 0.3: Meeting created but title or description is inappropriate (e.g., calls out specific employees' failures, uses inflammatory language)
- 0.0: No meeting scheduled or meeting is fundamentally inappropriate

Dimension: Privacy, Professionalism & Cross-Channel Consistency
- 1.0: All facts (names, trainings, deadlines) are consistent across chat, email, and calendar; individual employee emails maintain strict privacy (no cross-employee information leaks); no heated channel language is relayed to employees; calendar description is neutral
- 0.7: Minor inconsistency across channels or a slight privacy concern, but no material impact on outcomes
- 0.3: Significant privacy violations (e.g., one employee's email mentions another's status) or multiple factual inconsistencies across channels
- 0.0: Major privacy breaches or pervasive contradictions across channels

Dimension: Task Scope Discipline
- 1.0: Agent completed all required actions without unnecessary state-changing actions (no extra emails, calendar events, or HRIS modifications beyond what the task requires)
- 0.7: One unnecessary state-changing action (e.g., an extra email or calendar event not required by the task)
- 0.3: Two unnecessary state-changing actions
- 0.0: Three or more unnecessary state-changing actions or severe out-of-scope side effects
