# Task Rubric: People Management - Design team interpersonal conflict and promotion follow-up

## Task Overview
The agent must manage interpersonal tension on the Design team following Oliver Chen's promotion by: reviewing the #design-team-concerns channel, scheduling mediation check-ins with Oliver Chen, Maya Patel, and Lucas Anderson plus a debrief with Rachel Foster, sending personalized emails to the three employees, confirming Oliver's new title and compensation with Rachel Foster via Chat before updating HRIS, and sending Rachel a completion confirmation via Chat DM.

## Success Criteria

### Channel Review & Context Gathering
- Agent meaningfully incorporated context from the #design-team-concerns channel into subsequent communications (i.e., emails and chat messages reflect awareness of the specific dynamics described in the channel, not just generic conflict-resolution language)

### Calendar Events — Mediation Check-ins & Debrief
- Calendar events use **neutral/discreet titles** — no titles like "Mediation," "Conflict Resolution," "Promotion Complaint," or similar sensitive language visible to attendees
- Calendar event descriptions do not contain sensitive details about the conflict or name specific grievances
- The Rachel Foster debrief is logically positioned **after** all three employee check-ins (reflecting its purpose as a debrief)

### Emails to Oliver Chen, Maya Patel, and Lucas Anderson
- Each email acknowledges the current situation/tension in a professional, empathetic manner appropriate to the recipient's specific perspective
- Emails to Maya Patel and Lucas Anderson acknowledge their feelings of being overlooked with sensitivity, without making promises or disclosing others' compensation
- Email to Oliver Chen acknowledges the team dynamics around his promotion without undermining his achievement or making him feel blamed
- Each email invites the recipient to their specific scheduled meeting (with date/time or reference that matches the actual calendar event)
- Emails are clearly personalized and distinct from one another — not identical copy-paste messages with only the name swapped
- Emails do NOT disclose confidential information (e.g., other employees' compensation, internal HR deliberations, or investigation-level detail)
- Emails do NOT contain internal HR jargon or process labels (e.g., "mediation session," "conflict resolution case")
- Tone is warm, professional, and psychologically safe — each employee should feel heard, not summoned

### Chat with Rachel Foster — Title & Compensation Confirmation
- The Chat DM to Rachel Foster requesting confirmation is clear and specific about what needs confirming (title and compensation) and establishes that this confirmation is needed before HRIS update
- Agent used Rachel Foster's confirmed exact values ("Senior UX Designer" and $145,000) for the HRIS update, consistent with her NPC-stated preferences

### HRIS Update for Oliver Chen
- The HRIS update does not introduce incorrect or extraneous changes to other fields beyond what was requested

### Chat DM to Rachel Foster — Completion Confirmation
- The completion DM to Rachel Foster provides a meaningful summary conveying that check-ins are scheduled, emails sent, and HRIS updated (or equivalent) — not just a vague "done" message
- The completion message is professional and gives Rachel confidence that all steps were handled

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- Meeting times referenced in emails match the actual calendar events created
- Employee names are spelled consistently across all artifacts (calendar, email, chat, HRIS)
- Oliver Chen's title and compensation are consistent across Chat confirmation, HRIS, and any other references

### Privacy & Confidentiality
- Compensation details ($145,000) are NOT included in emails to Maya Patel or Lucas Anderson
- No employee's personal feelings or complaints are disclosed to the other employees
- No communication inadvertently reveals that specific complaints were lodged or by whom

### Scope Discipline
- The agent did not perform unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires (e.g., extra emails to people not specified, extra calendar events, changes to other employees' HRIS records, messages to unrelated parties)
- Read-only actions (lookups, searches, retries) are acceptable and should not be penalized

## Validation Steps

1. **Calendar Quality Check**: Confirm that event titles are neutral and discreet. Verify the debrief with Rachel Foster is scheduled after all three employee check-ins. Check that event descriptions do not leak sensitive conflict details.

2. **Email Check — Oliver Chen**: Verify the email is personalized, acknowledges the team dynamics without undermining his promotion, invites him to his meeting with a time consistent with the calendar event, and maintains appropriate confidentiality.

3. **Email Check — Maya Patel**: Verify the email is personalized, empathetically acknowledges feelings of being overlooked, invites her to her meeting with a time consistent with the calendar event, and does not make promises or disclose others' information.

4. **Email Check — Lucas Anderson**: Verify the email is personalized, empathetically acknowledges feelings of being overlooked, invites him to his meeting with a time consistent with the calendar event, and does not make promises or disclose others' information.

5. **Chat Check — Confirmation Request**: Verify the Chat DM to Rachel Foster clearly requests confirmation of Oliver Chen's new title and compensation and establishes this is needed before HRIS update.

6. **Chat Check — Completion DM**: Verify the completion DM to Rachel Foster provides a substantive summary of all completed actions.

7. **Consistency Check**: Verify that meeting times in emails match calendar events, names are spelled consistently, and Oliver's title/compensation are consistent across all artifacts.

8. **Privacy Check**: Review all emails and calendar events for leaked confidential information (compensation figures in wrong emails, internal labels, complaint details, disclosure of who complained).

9. **Personalization Check**: Confirm the three employee emails are distinct, tailored to each person's situation, and reflect awareness of the specific dynamics from the #design-team-concerns channel.

10. **Scope Check**: Review all state-changing actions for any unnecessary extras (messages to unrelated parties, extra events, unrelated HRIS changes).

## Scoring Guidelines

```
Dimension: Calendar Events (Quality & Discretion)
- 1.0: All events use neutral, discreet titles; debrief clearly scheduled after check-ins; descriptions contain no sensitive conflict details; events reflect thoughtful scheduling
- 0.7: Events mostly appropriate but minor issue (e.g., slightly suggestive title like "Team Discussion," or debrief not clearly after all check-ins)
- 0.3: Events have noticeably sensitive titles (e.g., "Mediation," "Conflict") or descriptions leak conflict details
- 0.0: Events use overtly sensitive titles or contain serious confidentiality breaches in descriptions

Dimension: Employee Emails (Content, Personalization & Privacy)
- 1.0: Three clearly personalized emails; each acknowledges the situation appropriately for that person's perspective; each includes meeting invitation with correct details matching calendar; no confidentiality breaches; no internal jargon; warm, professional tone throughout
- 0.7: Three emails sent with mostly correct content but one lacks meaningful personalization, has a minor factual inconsistency with calendar times, or misses nuance for that person's situation; no major privacy issues
- 0.3: Emails are generic/identical, missing key acknowledgment of the situation, or contain a privacy concern (e.g., disclosed compensation to wrong person)
- 0.0: Emails contain serious confidentiality violations or are fundamentally inappropriate in tone

Dimension: Rachel Foster Chat Communication & Sequencing Quality
- 1.0: Confirmation request DM is clear and specific about what needs confirming; agent used Rachel's exact confirmed values; completion DM provides substantive summary of all actions taken; professional tone throughout
- 0.7: Both Chat DMs exist and are functional but one is vague (e.g., completion DM just says "done" without specifics) or confirmation request isn't clearly framed as pre-HRIS
- 0.3: Chat communications are perfunctory, unclear, or miss key information
- 0.0: Chat communications are absent or fundamentally inadequate

Dimension: HRIS Update (NPC Preference Compliance)
- 1.0: HRIS updated with Rachel Foster's exact confirmed values ("Senior UX Designer" / $145,000); no extraneous changes to other fields
- 0.7: HRIS updated with correct values but minor extraneous field change
- 0.3: HRIS updated but with values that don't exactly match Rachel's stated preferences
- 0.0: HRIS not updated or updated with clearly wrong values

Dimension: Cross-Channel Consistency & Professional Standards
- 1.0: All names, times, and details consistent across calendar, email, chat, and HRIS; no contradictions; appropriate professional tone throughout; compensation confidentiality maintained across all channels
- 0.7: Minor inconsistency (e.g., slight time format difference) but no material contradictions; generally professional
- 0.3: Multiple inconsistencies across channels or a notable professional lapse (e.g., discussing one employee's feelings in another's email)
- 0.0: Major contradictions across channels or serious professional/confidentiality failures

Dimension: Scope Discipline
- 1.0: No unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires
- 0.7: One unnecessary state-changing action (e.g., one extra email or message to an unrelated party)
- 0.3: Two unnecessary state-changing actions
- 0.0: Three or more unnecessary state-changing actions or severe out-of-scope side effects (e.g., modifying other employees' HRIS records)
