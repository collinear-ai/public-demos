# Task Rubric: People Management - Resolve Conflicting DEI Training Accommodation Requests

## Task Overview
The agent must resolve a conflict between Emma Thompson and Rachel Foster regarding the format of a DEI workshop for Design employees. This requires gathering information via chat from Rachel Foster and Sarah Johnson before sending a resolution email to both parties, scheduling an alignment meeting, and sending a final summary DM to Sarah Johnson using her preferred framing.

## Success Criteria

### Pre-Action Information Gathering
- Agent reads existing emails from Emma Thompson and Rachel Foster to understand the conflict before taking action
- Chat DM to Rachel Foster is substantive — clearly asks her to confirm her directive still stands and requests her exact reasoning/justification for communicating to Emma (not a vague or generic message)
- Chat DM to Sarah Johnson is substantive — specifically asks for guidance on tone, language, and framing for cross-departmental communication to Talent Acquisition leadership (not a vague or generic message)
- Both information-gathering chats demonstrate the agent understood the conflict context before reaching out

### Resolution Email Quality & Content
- Email clearly states that **Rachel Foster's directive as Design Director takes precedence** over Emma's request, framed as organizational authority rather than personal preference
- Email includes Rachel's exact stated reasoning verbatim or near-verbatim: *"The Design Director has confirmed that live in-person attendance is mandatory for all Design team members because the interactive exercises in the DEI Foundations workshop are essential to building the cross-functional empathy our department's inclusion strategy depends on, and the asynchronous format does not replicate that experience."* — Rachel explicitly requested this exact wording be used
- Email tone is diplomatic, professional, and empathetic — acknowledges the potential tension and Emma's perspective rather than being dismissive or curt
- Email does not undermine either party, use inflammatory/judgmental language, or relay sensitive internal HR details inappropriately
- Email language is clear and resolution-oriented, helping to defuse tension rather than escalate it

### Calendar: DEI Training Format Alignment Meeting
- Meeting title is neutral and appropriate (e.g., "DEI Training Format Alignment" or semantically equivalent) — not inflammatory or overly vague
- Meeting is scheduled during the week of **2026-02-02 through 2026-02-06**

### Final Chat DM to Sarah Johnson
- The final DM to Sarah Johnson uses her exact preferred framing verbatim or near-verbatim: *"Flagging for Talent Acquisition leadership visibility: a format conflict between Design management levels on DEI Foundations training has been resolved in favor of the Design Director's mandatory in-person attendance policy. An alignment meeting has been scheduled between the parties."* — Sarah explicitly requested this specific framing
- The message is concise and neutral in tone, consistent with Sarah's stated preference for professional cross-departmental communication

### NPC Preference Compliance
- **Rachel Foster's preference**: Her exact reasoning statement is used in the email to Emma (verbatim or near-verbatim as she explicitly requested exact wording)
- **Sarah Johnson's preference**: Her exact framing language is used in the final DM (verbatim or near-verbatim as she explicitly requested specific framing)

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- The resolution communicated in the email matches what is summarized in the DM to Sarah — no contradictory details about who prevailed, what the policy is, or what was decided
- Meeting details referenced in any communication match the actual calendar event
- Names are spelled consistently across all channels

### Professional Standards
- No internal HR jargon or sensitive details are inappropriately shared in communications
- The agent defers to Rachel Foster's authority as Design Director — does not attempt to override or negotiate her decision
- Tone is professional and empathetic throughout, acknowledging the conflict without taking sides beyond recognizing organizational authority

### Scope Discipline
- Agent does not send unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires (resolution email to Emma and Rachel, calendar event, DMs to Rachel and Sarah for information gathering, final DM to Sarah)
- Extra read-only actions (searches, lookups, retries) are acceptable and should not be penalized
- Each confirmed unnecessary state-changing action (extra emails, extra calendar events, messages to uninvolved parties) should reduce the score

## Validation Steps

1. **Check information-gathering quality**: Verify that DMs to Rachel Foster and Sarah Johnson asked substantive, relevant questions (not generic or vague)
2. **Check resolution email content & tone**: Verify email states Rachel's authority, includes Rachel's exact reasoning, and is diplomatically worded with empathetic acknowledgment of the situation
3. **Check calendar event quality**: Verify meeting title is appropriate and neutral
4. **Check final DM to Sarah Johnson content**: Verify message uses Sarah's preferred framing language verbatim/near-verbatim
5. **Cross-channel consistency**: Verify resolution details, names, and meeting references are consistent across email, chat, and calendar
6. **Professional standards**: Verify no inappropriate disclosures, inflammatory language, or authority overrides
7. **Scope discipline**: Verify no unnecessary state-changing actions were taken

## Scoring Guidelines

```
Dimension: Pre-Action Information Gathering Quality
- 1.0: Both DMs are substantive and well-targeted; Rachel is clearly asked to confirm her directive and provide exact justification; Sarah is specifically asked for tone/language guidance for cross-departmental communication to TA leadership; questions demonstrate understanding of the conflict
- 0.7: Both DMs sent with relevant intent but questions are somewhat vague or generic, missing specificity about what information is needed
- 0.3: Only one DM has substantive content; the other is perfunctory or off-topic
- 0.0: DMs (if sent) contain no meaningful information-gathering questions

Dimension: Resolution Email Quality & NPC Compliance
- 1.0: Email clearly states Rachel's authority as Design Director takes precedence; includes Rachel's exact requested reasoning verbatim/near-verbatim; tone is diplomatic and empathetic — acknowledges tension and Emma's perspective; no inflammatory language, internal jargon, or inappropriate disclosures
- 0.7: Email states Rachel's precedence and includes reasoning, but reasoning is noticeably paraphrased rather than using Rachel's exact requested wording, OR tone is adequate but not particularly diplomatic/empathetic
- 0.3: Email communicates a resolution but is missing key quality elements (doesn't clearly frame as organizational authority, reasoning absent or significantly altered, tone is dismissive or curt)
- 0.0: No resolution email content addresses the conflict, or email undermines the resolution/takes the wrong side

Dimension: Calendar Meeting Quality
- 1.0: Meeting title is appropriate and neutral (e.g., "DEI Training Format Alignment" or equivalent); scheduled within the correct week
- 0.7: Meeting title is functional but significantly different from task specification, or minor scheduling issue
- 0.3: Meeting title is inappropriate/inflammatory, or event is missing key quality aspects
- 0.0: No meaningful calendar event quality to assess

Dimension: Final DM to Sarah Johnson — Preferred Framing
- 1.0: DM uses Sarah's exact preferred framing verbatim or near-verbatim, including key phrases ("Talent Acquisition leadership visibility," "format conflict between Design management levels," "mandatory in-person attendance policy," "alignment meeting has been scheduled between the parties"); message is concise and neutral
- 0.7: DM contains the core content of Sarah's preferred framing but noticeably paraphrased or missing one or more key phrases
- 0.3: DM gives a general update about the resolution but does not use Sarah's preferred framing
- 0.0: No final DM content to Sarah after resolution, or message content is about something else entirely

Dimension: Cross-Channel Consistency, Professional Standards & Scope Discipline
- 1.0: Resolution details consistent across all channels; professional tone throughout; correct authority deference; no privacy violations or inappropriate disclosures; no unnecessary state-changing actions beyond task requirements
- 0.7: Minor inconsistency or one unnecessary state-changing action; overall professional with no material contradictions
- 0.3: Material inconsistency across channels, a notable professional lapse, or two unnecessary state-changing actions
- 0.0: Major contradictions across channels, significant professional/privacy violations, or three+ unnecessary state-changing actions
