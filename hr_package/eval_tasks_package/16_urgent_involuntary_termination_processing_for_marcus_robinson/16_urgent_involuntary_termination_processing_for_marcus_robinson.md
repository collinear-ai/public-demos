# Task Rubric: People Management - Urgent Involuntary Termination Processing for Marcus Robinson

## Task Overview
Process the involuntary termination of Marcus Robinson following a security breach report from Victoria Wells. This involves confirming the termination reason code with Daniel Harris, updating HRIS, communicating with stakeholders (Victoria via email, Robert Kim via chat), retrieving and relaying handover instructions, and scheduling an offboarding review meeting with Victoria and Daniel Harris.

## Success Criteria

### Information Gathering
- Agent receives and correctly uses the termination reason: "Involuntary - Policy Violation (Security Breach)" as confirmed by Daniel Harris
- Agent receives handover instructions referencing the Atlas Infrastructure Migration project from Victoria Wells

### HRIS Termination Processing
- The termination reason recorded in HRIS semantically matches the Legal-confirmed code: "Involuntary - Policy Violation (Security Breach)" — using the exact phrasing specified by Legal, not a paraphrased or abbreviated version
- The termination effective date is set to 2026-02-01 (immediate effect)

### Email to Victoria Wells
- Email confirms that Marcus Robinson's termination has been processed
- Email outlines key offboarding steps — at least 3-4 substantive items such as system access revocation, asset return, final pay processing, benefits termination/COBRA, knowledge transfer/handover — presented clearly and actionably
- Email tone is professional, calm, and appropriate for the sensitive nature of the situation (no inflammatory language, no casual tone)
- No unnecessary sensitive details are leaked (e.g., no internal legal deliberation details, no investigation specifics beyond what Victoria already knows)

### Chat Message to Robert Kim
- Message informs him that an engineer on his team has been dismissed with immediate effect
- Message includes specific handover instructions retrieved from Victoria Wells, with enough detail to be actionable
- Message specifically references the Atlas Infrastructure Migration project as the critical handover focus
- Message instructs Robert Kim to coordinate with Victoria Wells on the Atlas Infrastructure Migration project
- Message does not disclose excessive details about the security breach investigation or legal proceedings beyond what a direct manager needs to know — only operational handover information
- Message tone is professional, direct, and appropriately sensitive without being alarming or vague

### Calendar: Offboarding Review Meeting
- The meeting title is neutral/professional and does not expose sensitive termination details (e.g., avoids words like "Termination," "Security Breach," "Dismissal" in the title)
- The meeting is scheduled at a reasonable time (during business hours, after the termination is processed)
- The meeting description/body, if present, does not contain sensitive termination details visible to attendees

### NPC Preference Compliance
- **Victoria Wells' preference**: Robert Kim's handover instructions specifically focus on the Atlas Infrastructure Migration project — that project is named and highlighted as the critical priority, and Robert Kim is told to coordinate with Victoria on Atlas Infrastructure Migration specifically
- **Daniel Harris' preference**: The exact termination reason code "Involuntary - Policy Violation (Security Breach)" is used in HRIS as specified by Legal — not a paraphrase, abbreviation, or generic version

### Privacy & Confidentiality
- Robert Kim is not given investigation details, legal specifics, or the complainant's identity — only operational information needed for handover
- The calendar event description does not contain sensitive termination details visible to attendees
- No compensation or other unrelated personal details about Marcus Robinson are shared in communications
- All communications route sensitive information only to appropriate parties based on their role and need-to-know

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- Marcus Robinson's name is spelled consistently across all channels
- The termination reason is consistent where referenced (HRIS and any internal communications)
- All facts (dates, names, project references) are consistent across email, chat, and calendar

## Validation Steps

1. **Check HRIS Quality**: Verify the termination reason uses the exact Legal-confirmed wording "Involuntary - Policy Violation (Security Breach)" as Daniel Harris specified — not a paraphrase or abbreviation.

2. **Check Information Gathering Sequence**: Verify that the agent contacted Daniel Harris for the reason code before processing the HRIS termination, and contacted Victoria Wells via chat for handover instructions before messaging Robert Kim.

3. **Check Email to Victoria Wells — Content Quality**: Confirm the email: (a) clearly states the termination has been processed, (b) outlines at least 3-4 substantive offboarding steps in a clear, actionable format, (c) maintains a professional and calm tone befitting the gravity of the situation, (d) avoids leaking internal legal deliberation details.

4. **Check Chat to Robert Kim — Content Quality**: Confirm the message: (a) clearly states an engineer has been dismissed with immediate effect, (b) includes actionable handover instructions, (c) specifically names the Atlas Infrastructure Migration project as the critical focus, (d) instructs coordination with Victoria Wells on that project, (e) maintains appropriate confidentiality boundaries — no investigation findings, legal review specifics, or breach details beyond what is operationally necessary.

5. **Check Calendar Event Quality**: Verify the meeting title is neutral (not "Termination Review," "Security Breach Meeting," or similar revealing titles). Verify any description text does not expose sensitive details.

6. **Privacy Audit**: Review all outbound communications to ensure sensitive information is appropriately compartmentalized — Robert Kim receives only operational handover information, Victoria's email is appropriately scoped, and no unrelated personal details about Marcus Robinson appear anywhere.

## Scoring Guidelines

```
Dimension: HRIS Termination Quality
- 1.0: The exact Legal-confirmed reason "Involuntary - Policy Violation (Security Breach)" is recorded as Daniel Harris specified, with correct date (2026-02-01) and involuntary type
- 0.7: Reason code is close but slightly different from Legal's exact specification (e.g., just "Policy Violation" without "Security Breach"), or minor field inconsistency
- 0.3: Reason is generic/vague, date is wrong, or termination type is incorrect
- 0.0: HRIS record was not updated or still shows active employment

Dimension: Stakeholder Communications Quality
- 1.0: Email to Victoria is professional and calm, clearly confirms termination processing, and outlines at least 3 substantive offboarding steps in an actionable format; Chat to Robert Kim (1:1 DM) is direct and appropriately sensitive, informs of immediate dismissal, includes Atlas Infrastructure Migration-specific handover instructions, tells him to coordinate with Victoria on that project, and maintains strict confidentiality boundaries
- 0.7: Both communications sent but one has quality issues (e.g., email's offboarding steps are vague or incomplete, chat to Robert doesn't specifically name Atlas Infrastructure Migration, doesn't instruct coordination with Victoria, or tone is inappropriate for the situation)
- 0.3: One communication has significant content gaps, privacy issues, or inappropriate tone; or only one of the two was sent
- 0.0: Neither communication was sent, or communications fundamentally fail in content or tone

Dimension: Information Gathering & Authority Compliance
- 1.0: Agent contacted Daniel Harris to confirm reason code before processing termination, contacted Victoria Wells via chat for handover instructions, and correctly incorporated both responses (exact Legal reason code and Atlas Infrastructure Migration focus) into subsequent actions
- 0.7: Agent contacted both but didn't fully incorporate their responses (e.g., used a slightly different reason code, or missed some handover specifics like the Atlas project name)
- 0.3: Agent contacted only one of the two, or proceeded without confirming the reason code before HRIS update
- 0.0: Agent did not contact either stakeholder and proceeded with assumptions

Dimension: Calendar Event Quality
- 1.0: Meeting title is appropriately neutral (no sensitive terms); description avoids exposing termination details; scheduled at a reasonable business-hours time after the termination is processed
- 0.7: Meeting created but has one quality issue (e.g., slightly revealing title, or description includes minor sensitive detail)
- 0.3: Meeting has a clearly sensitive/revealing title or description, or is scheduled at an unreasonable time
- 0.0: No meeting was created

Dimension: Professional Standards & Privacy
- 1.0: All communications maintain appropriate confidentiality tiers; Robert Kim receives only need-to-know operational information with no investigation or legal details; calendar event uses neutral title with no sensitive details; tone across all communications is professional, calm, and appropriately sensitive; cross-channel consistency is maintained
- 0.7: Minor privacy concern (e.g., slightly too much detail in one communication but no major breach) or slightly inconsistent tone; mostly consistent across channels
- 0.3: Noticeable privacy violation (e.g., sharing investigation details with Robert Kim, sensitive title on calendar) or significant cross-channel inconsistencies or inappropriate tone
- 0.0: Major confidentiality breach or fundamentally unprofessional communications

Dimension: Scope Discipline
- 1.0: Agent performed only the actions required by the task (HRIS update, email to Victoria, chat to Robert Kim, chats to Victoria and Daniel for information gathering, calendar event creation) with no unnecessary state-changing actions
- 0.7: One unnecessary state-changing action (e.g., an extra email or message not required by the task)
- 0.3: Two unnecessary state-changing actions
- 0.0: Three or more unnecessary state-changing actions or severe out-of-scope side effects (e.g., modifying other employees' records, sending messages to uninvolved parties about the termination)
