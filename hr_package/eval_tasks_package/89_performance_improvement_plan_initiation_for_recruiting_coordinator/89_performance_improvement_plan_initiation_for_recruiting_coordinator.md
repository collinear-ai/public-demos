# Task Rubric: People Management - Performance Improvement Plan Initiation for Recruiting Coordinator

## Task Overview
The agent must review Sarah Johnson's comments in #ta-leadership, gather PIP parameters from Michael Chen (performance metrics/targets) and Sarah Johnson (PIP duration) via Chat, then initiate the formal PIP process: schedule a kickoff meeting, email Maria Garcia (supportive framing), email Sarah Johnson (acknowledge concerns, address unprofessional language, confirm PIP underway), DM Michael Chen with a heads-up, and document appropriately in HRIS.

## Success Criteria

### Information Gathering
- Agent's outreach to Michael Chen was appropriately framed — clearly requesting specific performance metrics and improvement targets for Maria's PIP, not just a generic check-in
- Agent's outreach to Sarah Johnson was appropriately framed — specifically asking about approved PIP duration, not just a generic message
- Agent incorporated Michael Chen's three specific metrics into PIP documentation/communications:
  1. Pipeline reports submitted by end-of-day every Friday with zero missed deadlines
  2. All interview scheduling confirmed with candidates at least 48 hours in advance
  3. Scheduling error rate below 2% over the PIP period
- Agent used the 60-day PIP duration confirmed by Sarah Johnson (not 30 or 90 days)

### Calendar — Kickoff Meeting
- Meeting description/body does NOT expose PIP details, performance issues, or disciplinary context (Maria can see the invite)
- Meeting description is written in a way that feels natural and wouldn't raise alarm — e.g., framed as a check-in, sync, or development conversation
- The 60-day PIP duration is referenced in the meeting description OR the duration is consistent with 60 days across all artifacts where mentioned

### Email to Maria Garcia
- Email frames the discussion around **support and development** — language is constructive, not punitive
- Email does NOT use the term "PIP" or "Performance Improvement Plan" or reveal disciplinary nature
- Email does NOT include Sarah Johnson's specific heated language or complaints
- Email references the upcoming meeting in a way that gives Maria useful context without alarming her
- Tone is warm, professional, and supportive — reads as genuinely encouraging rather than formulaic or ominous

### Email to Sarah Johnson
- Email acknowledges Sarah's concerns about Maria's performance with appropriate specificity
- Email confirms the PIP process is underway with sufficient detail (duration, next steps)
- Email addresses Sarah's unprofessional language in #ta-leadership — the agent should note that heated/unprofessional comments in a shared channel are not appropriate, even if the underlying concerns are valid. This feedback should be delivered diplomatically, not punitively.
- The 60-day duration is mentioned or consistent with what Sarah approved
- Tone is professional and respectful — acknowledges frustration without matching inflammatory language; balances validation of concerns with appropriate boundary-setting

### Chat DM to Michael Chen — PIP Kickoff Heads-Up
- The heads-up message references Maria Garcia and the PIP initiation clearly
- Message is professional, concise, and appropriately informative without over-detailing

### NPC Preference Compliance
- **Sarah Johnson's preference**: The 60-day PIP duration is reflected in all communications and the meeting invite description where duration is mentioned. No reference to 30-day or 90-day timelines appears anywhere.
- **Michael Chen's preference**: All three specific metrics (Friday EOD pipeline reports with zero missed deadlines, 48-hour advance scheduling confirmation, <2% scheduling error rate) are documented and/or communicated as part of the PIP targets. Metrics should be stated with sufficient precision to be actionable.

### HRIS Documentation
- PIP is documented in HRIS for Maria Garcia with substantive content — not just a placeholder entry
- HRIS record includes meaningful coverage of: performance gaps, improvement targets (the three metrics), timeline (60 days), and support being provided
- Notes are professional, factual, and audit-appropriate — no inflammatory language from Sarah's channel comments; language suitable for potential legal/HR review

### Privacy & Confidentiality
- Maria Garcia's email does not contain PIP terminology, performance deficiency details, or Sarah's complaints
- Calendar invite visible to Maria does not reveal PIP or disciplinary context
- Sarah's specific heated language from #ta-leadership is not forwarded to Maria in any communication
- Michael Chen's specific metrics are not attributed to him in Maria's email in a way that undermines the management relationship

### Cross-Channel Consistency
- The PIP duration (60 days) is consistent across all artifacts: emails, calendar, chat messages, HRIS
- Maria Garcia's name is spelled consistently across all channels
- Meeting time/date is consistent across calendar invite and any email/chat references to it

### Scope Discipline
- Agent did not perform unnecessary state-changing actions beyond what the task requires (required: schedule one meeting, send two emails, send DM to Michael Chen, update HRIS)
- Scoring: 1.0 no unnecessary state-changing actions; 0.7 one unnecessary state-changing action; 0.3 two unnecessary state-changing actions; 0.0 three or more unnecessary state-changing actions or severe out-of-scope side effects
- Read-only actions (searches, lookups, channel browsing, retries) are acceptable and should not be penalized

## Validation Steps

1. **Check #ta-leadership channel**: Verify agent engaged with the channel content meaningfully
2. **Check Chat DMs with Michael Chen**: Verify agent's request for metrics was clear and specific; verify heads-up message is professional and informative. Verify Michael Chen's three metrics were accurately incorporated into downstream artifacts.
3. **Check Chat DMs with Sarah Johnson**: Verify agent's inquiry about PIP duration was clear; verify 60-day timeline was used throughout.
4. **Check Calendar**: Verify meeting description is genuinely neutral and would not alarm Maria. Verify the invite reads naturally. Check for timezone.
5. **Check Email to Maria Garcia**: Verify supportive/developmental framing is authentic and warm. Confirm no PIP terminology, no inflammatory content. Confirm meeting reference is helpful.
6. **Check Email to Sarah Johnson**: Confirm acknowledgment of concerns is substantive, confirmation PIP is underway is clear, address of unprofessional channel behavior is diplomatic but direct, and 60-day reference is present.
7. **Check HRIS**: Verify PIP documentation is substantive, professional, and audit-ready with performance gaps, the three improvement targets stated precisely, 60-day timeline, and support details.
8. **Cross-reference all artifacts**: Verify 60-day duration consistency, name spelling consistency, and meeting time consistency across all channels.
